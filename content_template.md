## Writing Guidelines

### What Makes Content "Quality"

1. **Specific numbers over vague statements**
   - ❌ "A small amount can make a big difference"
   - ✅ "A 5% increase in insulation thickness reduces heat loss by 12-18%"

2. **Real scenarios over abstract explanations**
   - ❌ "Enter the dimensions of your room"
   - ✅ "For a 4×5 meter room with 2.7m ceilings, enter 4 in Length and 5 in Width"

3. **Show consequences**
   - ❌ "Make sure to measure accurately"
   - ✅ "A 2cm error at 5m length means ordering 10% too little material — not enough to complete the job"

4. **Address edge cases**
   - Mention when the formula doesn't apply
   - Note population-specific considerations (age, region, profession)
   - Flag common "gotchas" users encounter

5. **Unique perspective**
   - Include industry standards or regulations where relevant
   - Reference real products, materials, or practices
   - Add context that generic calculators don't provide

### Word Count Targets

| Section | Target |
|---------|--------|
| What is | 150-200 words |
| Formula | 150-200 words |
| Step-by-step | 100-150 words |
| Examples | 200-300 words |
| Mistakes | 150-200 words |
| Pro Tips | 150-200 words |
| FAQ (4 questions) | 400-500 words |
| **Total** | **1,300-1,700 words** |

### SEO Best Practices

- Use the calculator name (and common synonyms) naturally throughout
- Include "how to calculate [X]" phrasing
- Answer "what is [X]" clearly in the first section
- FAQ questions should match actual Google searches
- Link to 3-5 related calculators
