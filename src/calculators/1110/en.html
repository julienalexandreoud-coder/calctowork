This calculator helps you determine the exact quantity of floor tiles, adhesive, and grout needed for your project.

How to Use This Calculator

Follow these simple steps to get accurate results:

  1. Joint length per m²: 2 × (30+30) ÷ (30×30) = 0.133 linear m per cm² → 1.33 m/m²
  2. Joint cross-section: 3 mm × 8 mm = 24 mm²
  3. Volume per m²: 1.33 × 0.024 = 0.032 litres ≈ 0.058 kg/m² for 20 m² = 1.16 kg
  4. Standard grout ≈ 1.7 kg/m² for 30×30 tile — total for 20 m² = 34 kg → 7 × 5 kg bags

How It Works

The calculator divides your total floor area by the area of a single tile to determine the base number needed. It automatically adds a waste margin (typically 10%) to account for cutting losses at edges and corners, and calculates material quantities for adhesive and grout based on industry standards.

Input Fields

  • Tile length
  • Tile width
  • Total tiled area
  • Grout joint width

Result Outputs

  • Grout needed
  • 5 kg bags
  • 10 kg bags
  • Coverage per kg

Formula

Grout per m² = 2 × (L+W) ÷ (L×W) × grout_width × tile_thickness × grout_density

Helpful Tips

  • Always order 10-15% extra material for cutting waste and future repairs
  • Verify all tiles come from the same manufacturing batch to ensure color consistency
  • Plan your starting position to minimize edge cuts and create a balanced layout
  • Use thin-set mortar for interior floors and flexible adhesive for moisture-prone areas
  • Allow 24-48 hours for adhesive to cure completely before applying grout
