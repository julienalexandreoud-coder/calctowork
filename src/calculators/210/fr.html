How to calculate the area of a circle

The area of a circle is the surface enclosed within the circumference. You need the radius (center to edge) or the diameter (twice the radius). This calculator returns the area, circumference, and diameter instantly.

Circle area formula

Area = π × r²

Where r is the radius and π ≈ 3.14159. If you only have the diameter: Area = π × (d ÷ 2)²

Circumference: C = 2 × π × r

Practical example

Installing a circular pool with a 4 m diameter:

  1. Radius: 4 ÷ 2 = 2 m
  2. Area: π × 2² ≈ 12.57 m²
  3. Circumference: 2 × π × 2 ≈ 12.57 m

Applications

  • Construction: Area of circular slabs or foundations.
  • Gardening: Surface of circular flowerbeds or ponds.
  • Engineering: Cross-section of pipes and columns.
  • Math: Geometry problems in school and university.
