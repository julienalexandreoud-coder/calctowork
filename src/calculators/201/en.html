What is percentage change?

Percentage change measures how much a quantity has increased or decreased relative to its original value. A positive result means an increase; a negative result means a decrease. It's used to compare prices, financial results, statistics, and any measurable quantity that changes over time.

Percentage change formula

Change % = ((New Value − Original Value) ÷ Original Value) × 100

Step-by-step examples

Example 1 – Price increase: An apartment was worth $180,000 and is now $220,000.

  1. Difference: 220,000 − 180,000 = $40,000
  2. Change: (40,000 ÷ 180,000) × 100 = +22.2%

Example 2 – Stock drop: A stock falls from $50 to $38.

  1. Difference: 38 − 50 = −$12
  2. Change: (−12 ÷ 50) × 100 = −24%

Common uses

  • Finance: Investment returns, stock price movements.
  • Economics: Monthly inflation, GDP growth, unemployment rate changes.
  • Business: Quarter-over-quarter sales comparison.
  • Science: Population growth, temperature variation measurement.
