What are daily calorie needs?

Daily calorie needs (TDEE — Total Daily Energy Expenditure) represent the total energy your body burns in a day, including basal metabolism and all physical activity. Knowing your TDEE is essential for losing weight, gaining muscle, or maintaining your current weight in a controlled way.

Mifflin-St Jeor formula

The most scientifically accurate formula for Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR):

Men: BMR = 10 × weight(kg) + 6.25 × height(cm) − 5 × age + 5

Women: BMR = 10 × weight(kg) + 6.25 × height(cm) − 5 × age − 161

Activity multipliers

  • Sedentary (no exercise): BMR × 1.2
  • Light (1–3 days/week): BMR × 1.375
  • Moderate (3–5 days/week): BMR × 1.55
  • Active (6–7 days/week): BMR × 1.725
  • Very active (intense daily exercise): BMR × 1.9

Using TDEE for your goals

  • Lose weight: Eat 300–500 kcal below TDEE. Gradual deficit = sustainable loss.
  • Gain muscle: Eat 200–300 kcal above TDEE. Controlled surplus minimizes fat gain.
  • Maintain weight: Eat exactly your TDEE.
