This calculator determines the correct electrical cable size, voltage drop, and capacity for your installation based on current, distance, and system voltage.

How to Use This Calculator

Follow these simple steps to get accurate results:

  1. Calculate intensity total: 9200W ÷ 230V = 40A
  2. Apply factor simultaneidad: 40A × 0.6 = 24A
  3. Select IGA: 40A (per power contratada)
  4. Sección cable input: 10mm² (soporta 50A)

How It Works

The calculator applies standard electrical formulas to compute the minimum cable cross-section needed to safely carry the specified current while keeping voltage drop within acceptable limits. It accounts for copper or aluminum conductors and recommends standard commercial cable sizes.

Input Fields

  • Contracted power (kW)
  • Voltage
  • Diversity factor

Result Outputs

  • Maximum current
  • Recommended main breaker (A)
  • Estimated circuits

Formula

Intensidad = power ÷ voltage; I_simultánea = I_total × factor

Helpful Tips

  • Always size cables based on the maximum continuous load, not just average usage
  • Voltage drop should not exceed 3% for branch circuits and 5% for main feeders
  • Use copper conductors for better conductivity and longevity compared to aluminum
  • Install cable in conduit to protect against damage and improve cooling
  • Follow local electrical codes and regulations for your region
