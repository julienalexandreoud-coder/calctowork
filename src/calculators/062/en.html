Whether you are renovating a single room or planning a whole house build, getting your interior door quantities and hardware right is crucial for both budget and timeline. The Interior Door Calculator is a free online tool that instantly computes the total area of door leaves, the number of frames, handles, and hinges needed, as well as the perimeter and framing material for your project. By entering just three key measurements, you can move from guesswork to a precise materials list in seconds.

What the Interior Door Calculator Does and When to Use It

This calculator is designed for anyone involved in construction, renovation, or interior design who needs to order multiple interior doors. Instead of manually multiplying dimensions and adding hardware, you input the number of doors, their width, and height. The tool then outputs the square meters of each door leaf and the total for all doors, the number of frames required, the number of handles (two per door), the number of hinges based on door height, the total perimeter of all doors with a 5% waste allowance, and the quantity of framing material (cerco) needed for installation.

You should use this calculator when:

  • You are ordering doors for a new construction project and need a bulk materials list.
  • You want to estimate hardware costs before starting a renovation.
  • You are comparing material needs for different door sizes or quantities.
  • You need to quickly calculate waste-adjusted perimeter lengths for framing.
  • You are a contractor or DIY homeowner preparing a supply list for a subcontractor.

The Formula Explained Variable by Variable

The calculator uses a straightforward set of formulas based on standard construction practices. Each variable is defined clearly so you can understand exactly how the numbers are derived.

Input Variables

  • num_puertas (n): The total number of doors you plan to install.
  • ancho_m (W): The width of each door in meters. Default is 0.825 m (equivalent to approximately 32.5 inches, a common interior door width).
  • alto_m (H): The height of each door in meters. Default is 2.03 m (equivalent to approximately 80 inches, a standard interior door height).

Calculated Outputs

  1. m2_hoja (Door leaf area): W × H. This is the surface area of a single door leaf in square meters. For example, a door 0.825 m wide by 2.03 m high gives 1.67 m² per leaf.
  2. m2_total_hojas (Total door leaf area): m2_hoja × n. The combined surface area of all door leaves.
  3. marcos_ud (Number of frames): Equal to n. Each door requires one frame.
  4. tiradores (Number of handles): n × 2. Standard practice uses two handles per door (one on each side).
  5. bisagras (Number of hinges): Math.ceil(H / 0.3) × n. Hinges are placed every 0.3 meters (30 cm) along the door height, rounded up to the whole number. For a 2.03 m door, 2.03 / 0.3 = 6.77, rounded up to 7 hinges per door.
  6. perimetro (Total perimeter with waste): 2 × (W + H) × 1.05 × n. The perimeter of one door is 2 × (width + height). A 5% waste factor (multiply by 1.05) accounts for cuts and errors, then multiplied by the number of doors.
  7. cerco (Framing material): perimetro × 0.07. The perimeter length multiplied by 0.07 (7% of the perimeter) gives an estimate of the framing material needed, typically in linear meters of a specific profile.

Worked Examples with Concrete Numbers

Example 1: Standard Bedroom Doors for a Small House

Scenario: You are installing 6 standard interior doors, each 0.825 m wide and 2.03 m high.

  • Number of doors (n): 6
  • Width (W): 0.825 m
  • Height (H): 2.03 m

Step-by-step calculation:

  1. Door leaf area per door: 0.825 × 2.03 = 1.6748 → 1.67 m² (rounded).
  2. Total door leaf area: 1.67 × 6 = 10.02 m².
  3. Frames needed: 6.
  4. Handles needed: 6 × 2 = 12.
  5. Hinges per door: 2.03 / 0.3 = 6.77 → rounded up to 7. Total hinges: 7 × 6 = 42.
  6. Total perimeter with waste: 2 × (0.825 + 2.03) × 1.05 × 6 = 2 × 2.855 × 1.05 × 6 = 5.71 × 6.3 = 36.0 m (rounded to one decimal).
  7. Framing material: 36.0 × 0.07 = 2.52 m.

Result: For 6 doors, order 10.02 m² of door leaves, 6 frames, 12 handles, 42 hinges, 36.0 m of perimeter trim, and 2.52 m of framing profile.

Example 2: Large Doors for a Commercial Space

Scenario: You need 3 wide doors for an office, each 1.00 m wide and 2.30 m high.

  • Number of doors (n): 3
  • Width (W): 1.00 m (about 39 inches)
  • Height (H): 2.30 m (about 90.5 inches)

Step-by-step calculation:

  1. Door leaf area per door: 1.00 × 2.30 = 2.30 m².
  2. Total door leaf area: 2.30 × 3 = 6.90 m².
  3. Frames needed: 3.
  4. Handles needed: 3 × 2 = 6.
  5. Hinges per door: 2.30 / 0.3 = 7.67 → rounded up to 8. Total hinges: 8 × 3 = 24.
  6. Total perimeter with waste: 2 × (1.00 + 2.30) × 1.05 × 3 = 2 × 3.30 × 1.05 × 3 = 6.60 × 3.15 = 20.8 m.
  7. Framing material: 20.8 × 0.07 = 1.46 m.

Result: For 3 large doors, order 6.90 m² of door leaves, 3 frames, 6 handles, 24 hinges, 20.8 m of perimeter trim, and 1.46 m of framing material.

Common Mistakes When Using the Interior Door Calculator

1. Confusing Door Width with Rough Opening Width

This calculator requires the actual door leaf width, not the rough opening width. The rough opening is typically 2 to 5 cm (0.8 to 2 inches) wider than the door itself. If you enter the opening width, your calculations will be slightly off, especially the perimeter and framing material. Always measure the door slab itself, or use the specification sheet from your door supplier.

2. Forgetting the 5% Waste Factor for Perimeter

The waste factor (1.05 multiplier) is included specifically because you will lose material to cuts, miter joints, and mistakes. Some users try to calculate without this factor and end up short of trim material. Trust the 5% addition—it is a standard industry practice and saves you a second trip to the store.

3. Using Imperial Units Incorrectly

The calculator expects metric meters. If your measurements are in feet and inches, convert them before entering. For reference, 1 foot = 0.3048 meters, and 1 inch = 0.0254 meters. A common error is to type a value like 80 instead of 2.03 for a door height in inches—this will give absurdly large results. Double-check your units.

4. Misunderstanding the Hinge Count

The hinge calculation divides the door height by 0.3 m (30 cm) and rounds up. This ensures even spacing, but a door of exactly 2.1 m would give 7 hinges (2.1 / 0.3 = 7). Some users assume a fixed number like 3 or 4 hinges per door, but taller doors require more. Trust the calculator's count for proper structural support.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. Can I use this calculator for exterior doors?

This tool is optimized for interior doors. Exterior doors often have different hardware requirements (for example, they may use a single handle with a deadbolt instead of two handles, and they may require different hinge spacing or weatherstripping). While the leaf area and perimeter formulas are still valid, the hardware counts (handles, hinges) should not be applied to exterior doors without adjustment.

2. I have doors with different sizes. Can I still use this calculator?

The calculator is designed for one uniform door size per calculation. If you have multiple sizes, you should run the calculator separately for each door size. For example, run it once for your 0.825 m wide bedroom doors, and again for your 0.900 m wide closet doors. Then simply add the totals from each run to get your complete materials list.

3. What does "cerco" mean, and do I always need it?

"Cerco" refers to the framing or casing material used to install the door frame within the wall opening. It is typically a wood or MDF profile. In many construction systems, this is a standard requirement for finishing the door installation. The calculator provides an estimate of linear meters needed, but your specific framing system may have different requirements. Always confirm with your door installation guide or contractor.
