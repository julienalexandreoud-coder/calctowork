Speed, distance, and time relationship

The speed, distance, and time calculator solves any of the three quantities when you know the other two. It's one of the most fundamental formulas in classical physics, with direct applications in driving, cycling, running, physics, and astronomy.

The three formulas

  • Speed: v = d ÷ t
  • Distance: d = v × t
  • Time: t = d ÷ v

Practical examples

Example 1 – Speed: traveled 150 miles in 2.5 hours?

  1. v = 150 ÷ 2.5 = 60 mph

Example 2 – Time: how long at 60 mph to cover 240 miles?

  1. t = 240 ÷ 60 = 4 hours

Speed unit conversions

  • 1 mph = 1.609 km/h
  • 1 km/h = 0.6214 mph
  • 1 m/s = 3.6 km/h = 2.237 mph
  • 1 knot = 1.852 km/h (nautical and aviation)
