How to calculate net salary

Net salary is what you actually receive after income tax and social security deductions are applied to your gross salary. The difference between gross and net can be significant depending on your tax bracket and personal situation.

Typical deductions from gross salary

  • Social Security / National Insurance: Typically 6–12% depending on country.
  • Income Tax / PAYE: Progressive rate based on earnings bracket and personal allowances.
  • Pension contributions: May be deducted pre-tax, reducing taxable income.

How to increase your net salary

  • Use pre-tax benefits: health insurance, commuter benefits, childcare vouchers.
  • Maximize pension contributions (reduces taxable income).
  • Claim all eligible deductions and tax credits.
  • Consider salary sacrifice schemes where available.
