The Mosaic Calculator is a free online tool that helps you accurately estimate the materials needed for any mosaic tiling project. Whether you are laying a decorative backsplash, a custom floor medallion, or a full wall of glass mosaic tiles, this calculator saves you time and prevents costly over- or under-ordering by converting your project area into precise quantities of mosaic sheets, adhesive, and grout.

What the Mosaic Calculator Does and When to Use It

The calculator is designed for anyone working with mosaic tiles mounted on mesh sheets (typically 30 cm x 30 cm sheets, though you can adjust this size). It takes two simple inputs: the total area you want to cover (in square metres) and the size of each mosaic sheet (in centimetres). Using a standard 10% waste factor for cuts, breakage, and pattern adjustments, it then calculates three key outputs:

  • Total area with waste (m²) – your original area plus 10% extra.
  • Number of mosaic sheets needed – always rounded up so you never run short.
  • Adhesive weight (kg) – based on an average coverage rate of 5 kg per square metre.
  • Grout weight (kg) – based on an average coverage rate of 0.6 kg per square metre.

Use this calculator at the planning stage of any mosaic project, from small craft pieces to large commercial installations. It is especially helpful when buying materials online, as it gives you concrete numbers to place accurate orders without guesswork.

The Formula Explained Variable by Variable

The calculator uses a straightforward sequence of calculations. Here is what each variable means and how it contributes to the final results.

Input Variables

  • area (A) – The total surface you intend to tile, measured in square metres. This is the core input.
  • tam_malla_cm (tam) – The side length of each mosaic sheet in centimetres. The default is 30 (common for mesh-mounted mosaics), but you can change it if your sheets are larger or smaller.

Calculated Variables

  • area_merma – The area including a 10% waste buffer. It is computed as A × 1.10 and then rounded to two decimals. This covers accidental breakage, cuts needed for edges, and pattern mismatches.
  • malla_m2 – The area of one mosaic sheet in square metres. Since the sheet side is given in centimetres, first convert to metres: tam / 100. Then square that number: (tam/100) × (tam/100). For a 30 cm sheet, this is 0.09 m².
  • mallas (mallas_ud) – The total number of whole mosaic sheets required. It is Math.ceil(area_merma / malla_m2), which always rounds up to the next integer. It is always better to have a few extra than to fall short.
  • adhesivo (adhesivo_kg) – The amount of tile adhesive in kilograms. It is area_merma × 5, rounded to one decimal. The factor 5 kg/m² is a typical coverage for notched trowels used with mosaics.
  • lechada (lechada_kg) – The amount of grout in kilograms. It is area_merma × 0.6, rounded to one decimal. The factor 0.6 kg/m² works well for standard 2 mm grout joints.

If the area is missing or zero, the calculator returns an error so you always get valid results only when meaningful data is entered.

Two Worked Examples with Concrete Numbers

Example 1: Kitchen Backsplash (2.5 m², Standard 30 cm Sheets)

Input: area = 2.5 m², tam_malla_cm = 30

  1. Area with waste: 2.5 × 1.10 = 2.75 m²
  2. Sheet area: (30/100) × (30/100) = 0.09 m²
  3. Number of sheets: 2.75 / 0.09 = 30.56 → rounded up to 31 sheets
  4. Adhesive needed: 2.75 × 5 = 13.75 kg → round to 13.8 kg
  5. Grout needed: 2.75 × 0.6 = 1.65 kg → round to 1.7 kg

Result: For a 2.5 m² backsplash, you need 31 mosaic sheets (30 cm each), approximately 13.8 kg of adhesive, and 1.7 kg of grout. This leaves you with a comfortable 10% surplus for cuts.

Example 2: Small Bathroom Floor (4.2 m², Custom 40 cm Sheets)

Input: area = 4.2 m², tam_malla_cm = 40

  1. Area with waste: 4.2 × 1.10 = 4.62 m²
  2. Sheet area: (40/100) × (40/100) = 0.16 m²
  3. Number of sheets: 4.62 / 0.16 = 28.875 → rounded up to 29 sheets
  4. Adhesive needed: 4.62 × 5 = 23.1 kg
  5. Grout needed: 4.62 × 0.6 = 2.772 kg → round to 2.8 kg

Result: For a 4.2 m² floor using larger 40 cm sheets, you need 29 sheets, 23.1 kg of adhesive, and 2.8 kg of grout. Note that using bigger sheets reduces the number of pieces but does not change the adhesive and grout estimates per square metre.

Common Mistakes When Using the Mosaic Calculator

Even a simple tool can lead to errors if key details are overlooked. Here are the most frequent pitfalls and how to avoid them.

  • Mixing up units: The calculator expects area in square metres and sheet size in centimetres. If you enter area in square feet or sheet size in inches, the results will be wrong. Convert everything to metric first. For quick reference: 1 m² ≈ 10.764 ft², and 1 cm ≈ 0.394 inches. A 30 cm sheet is roughly 12 inches.
  • Ignoring the waste factor: Some users reduce the 10% waste because they think they are careful. This is risky. Mosaics break easily during cutting, especially glass and stone. The 10% accounts for these losses, as well as odd-shaped areas where you need partial sheets. Never lower the waste buffer.
  • Rounding down the number of sheets: The calculator automatically rounds up, but if you manually reduce the sheet count to save money, you may end up short. A missing sheet can delay a project for days. Always buy the rounded-up number, and return unused full sheets if possible.
  • Forgetting to adjust sheet size: Not all mosaic sheets are 30 cm. Some are 30.5 x 30.5 cm, others are 20 x 20 cm or custom sizes. Always measure your actual sheet dimensions and enter them. A wrong sheet size throws off the count significantly.
  • Overlooking grout and adhesive coverage: The factors of 5 kg/m² for adhesive and 0.6 kg/m² for grout are averages. Deep tiles, wide joints, or highly porous stone may require more. Use the calculator as a baseline, and always check product data sheets for specific coverage rates. For imperial users, 5 kg/m² is roughly 1.02 lb/ft², and 0.6 kg/m² is about 0.12 lb/ft².

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use this calculator for irregularly shaped areas like circles or L-shaped rooms?

Yes. Measure the total area of your irregular space as accurately as possible. For circles, use the formula π × radius². For L-shapes, break the area into rectangles, calculate each, and sum them. Then enter the total area into the calculator. The 10% waste factor will naturally cover extra cuts needed for curves and odd corners.

What if my mosaic tiles are not on 30 cm sheets, or if I am using individual loose tiles?

If your tiles come on sheets of a different size (e.g., 25 cm or 40 cm), simply change the "tam_malla_cm" input to match. For loose tiles not pre-mounted on mesh, you can still use the calculator by treating one sheet as a group of tiles that cover the sheet area. For example, if each loose tile is 2 cm x 2 cm and you lay them in a 15 x 15 grid, that grid is effectively a 30 cm x 30 cm sheet. Enter the grid size in centimetres. The calculator then estimates the needed number of those grids.

Why does the calculator round up the number of sheets, and can I order exactly the calculated number?

Rounding up ensures you have enough material to complete the job. Cutting mosaic sheets almost always leaves partial sheets, and you cannot buy fractions of a sheet. Ordering the rounded-up number guarantees that even with breakage and complicated cuts, you will not run out. Most suppliers allow returns of unopened full sheets, so buying a few extra is a safe and cost-effective strategy. Never try to order the exact decimal value from the division – it does not reflect real-world pack sizes.
