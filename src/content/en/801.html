What is Weight Converter?

The Weight Converter instantly transforms weight measurements between metric units (kilograms, grams, metric tons) and imperial/US units (pounds, ounces, stones). Whether you're tracking your weight loss progress (converting 75 kg to 165 lbs for an American app), shipping a package internationally (converting 5 lbs to 2.27 kg for customs forms), cooking from a foreign recipe (converting 8 oz to 227 grams), or calculating body weight for medication dosing, this calculator provides instant, accurate conversions. The converter uses precise, internationally-agreed conversion factors: 1 kilogram = 2.20462 pounds (exact), 1 pound = 16 ounces = 0.453592 kilograms. Enter any weight value, select your units, and get results in all common weight systems simultaneously — no need to calculate multiple times. Understanding weight conversions is essential for international travel, online shopping, fitness tracking, cooking, science labs, and any situation where measurements cross between metric and imperial systems.

How Weight Converter Works: Conversion Factors Explained

Weight conversion uses exact multiplication factors defined by international agreement. Key conversion factors: 1 kilogram = 2.20462 pounds (exact by definition since 1959). 1 pound = 0.45359237 kilograms (exact). 1 ounce = 28.3495 grams. 1 stone (UK only) = 14 pounds = 6.35029 kilograms. Converting kg to lbs: Multiply kilograms by 2.20462. Example: 70 kg × 2.20462 = 154.32 lbs. A 70 kg person weighs about 154 pounds. Converting lbs to kg: Divide pounds by 2.20462 (or multiply by 0.453592). Example: 180 lbs ÷ 2.20462 = 81.65 kg. An 180-pound person weighs about 82 kg. Converting ounces: 1 ounce = 1/16 of a pound = 28.35 grams. Example: 12 oz (a soda can) = 12 × 28.35 = 340 grams. Stone (UK weight): British people often state weight in stones and pounds. Example: "11 stone 4 pounds" = (11 × 14) + 4 = 158 lbs = 71.67 kg. Americans rarely use stones; this unit is primarily UK/Ireland for body weight.

Real-World Examples

Example 1 — Weight Loss Tracking: Sarah is European living in the US. Her goal is 65 kg, but her American fitness app uses pounds. Target in lbs: 65 × 2.20462 = 143.3 lbs. She sets her app goal to 143 lbs. Current weight: 78 kg = 172 lbs. She needs to lose 172 - 143 = 29 lbs (or 78 - 65 = 13 kg). Her weekly goal: lose 0.5 kg = 1.1 lbs per week — a healthy, sustainable rate.

Example 2 — International Shipping: You're mailing a package from the US to Germany. The package weighs 3 lbs 8 oz. First convert to decimal pounds: 3 + (8/16) = 3.5 lbs. Convert to kg: 3.5 ÷ 2.20462 = 1.588 kg. Customs form requires kg: declare 1.59 kg. Shipping cost: USPS charges by pounds, Deutsche Post charges by kg. Compare: 3.5 lbs × $4/lb = $14 via USPS vs. 1.59 kg × €8/kg = €12.72 (~$13.75) via Deutsche Post — nearly identical.

Example 3 — Cooking from British Recipe: A UK recipe calls for "1 lb 2 oz of flour." Convert: 1 lb + 2 oz = 16 oz + 2 oz = 18 oz total. In grams: 18 × 28.35 = 510.3 grams. Your kitchen scale shows grams, so measure 510g of flour. For baking, this precision matters — 510g vs. eyeballing "about a pound" can mean the difference between perfect bread and a dense brick.

Example 4 — Medication Dosage: Doctor prescribes medication at 5 mg per kg body weight. Patient weighs 176 lbs. First convert to kg: 176 ÷ 2.20462 = 79.83 kg. Dosage: 79.83 × 5 = 399.15 mg. Round to 400 mg for practical dosing. Getting this conversion wrong could mean underdosing (ineffective treatment) or overdosing (dangerous side effects). Always convert weight accurately for medical calculations.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Confusing kg and lbs by factor of 2.2: The most common error is forgetting which direction to convert. Converting kg to lbs should give a BIGGER number (multiply by 2.2). Converting lbs to kg should give a SMALLER number (divide by 2.2). If you convert 70 kg and get 32 lbs, you divided when you should have multiplied — the result is 2.2× too small. Sanity check: kg numbers are always smaller than equivalent lbs numbers.
  • Forgetting ounces are 1/16 of a pound: 8 oz is NOT 0.8 lbs — it's 0.5 lbs (half a pound). 4 oz = 0.25 lbs, 12 oz = 0.75 lbs. To convert oz to decimal lbs: divide by 16. Example: 5 lbs 9 oz = 5 + (9/16) = 5.5625 lbs total. Then convert to kg: 5.5625 ÷ 2.20462 = 2.52 kg.
  • Using stones incorrectly: Stones are ONLY used for body weight in UK/Ireland. Never use stones for packages, cooking, or other weights. Also, "10.5 stone" is ambiguous — does it mean 10 stone 5 lbs (145 lbs) or 10 stone 7 lbs (147 lbs, since 0.5 stone = 7 lbs)? Always write "10 stone 7 lbs" clearly, not "10.5 stone."
  • Rounding too early in medical/scientific contexts: For medication dosages or lab measurements, keep full precision until the final result. Converting 185 lbs to kg: 185 ÷ 2.20462 = 83.9146 kg. If you round to 84 kg too early, a 5 mg/kg dosage becomes 420 mg instead of 419.57 mg — usually insignificant, but for potent medications or small patients, precision matters.

Pro Tips for Better Results

  • Memorize benchmark conversions: Quick references: 1 kg ≈ 2.2 lbs; 50 kg = 110 lbs; 70 kg = 154 lbs; 100 kg = 220 lbs. For pounds to kg: 100 lbs = 45 kg; 150 lbs = 68 kg; 200 lbs = 91 kg. These benchmarks let you estimate quickly and verify calculator results are reasonable. If your calculator says 70 kg = 35 lbs, you know something's wrong.
  • Use the "add 10%" mental shortcut: For quick kg-to-lbs estimates: kg × 2 = double it, then add 10%. Example: 75 kg → 75 × 2 = 150 → 150 + 15 = 165 lbs. Actual: 165.35 lbs — nearly exact! For lbs-to-kg: lbs ÷ 2, then subtract 10%. Example: 180 lbs → 180 ÷ 2 = 90 → 90 - 9 = 81 kg. Actual: 81.65 kg. Close enough for quick estimates.
  • Understand regional weight conventions: US: body weight in lbs, food in lbs/oz, shipping in lbs. UK: body weight in stones+lbs, food in grams, shipping in kg. Europe/Asia: everything in kg/grams. When communicating internationally, know your audience. Telling a British friend you weigh "165 lbs" means less than "11 stone 11 lbs." Telling a German doctor you weigh "11 stone" will confuse them — use kg.
  • Check airline baggage limits in correct units: International flights often specify baggage in kg (23 kg checked, 8 kg carry-on). US domestic flights use lbs (50 lbs checked, 18 lbs carry-on). Convert BEFORE arriving at the airport. 23 kg = 50.7 lbs — so a 50-lb bag is just under the international limit. Weigh your bag at home in the correct units to avoid surprise fees.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why is 1 kg exactly 2.20462 lbs?

In 1959, the US, UK, Canada, Australia, New Zealand, and South Africa agreed to the International Yard and Pound Agreement, defining 1 pound = 0.45359237 kg EXACTLY. This made 1 kg = 2.20462262 lbs (the reciprocal). Before 1959, US and UK pounds differed slightly (by about 0.00001 kg — negligible for most purposes but significant for precision science). The agreement unified the pound across English-speaking countries and tied it to the metric standard. So 2.20462 isn't an approximation — it's derived from an exact, treaty-defined conversion factor.

What's the difference between weight and mass?

Technically, mass (kg) measures the amount of matter in an object — constant everywhere in the universe. Weight (lbs, or Newtons in physics) measures gravitational force on that mass — varies by location. On Earth, 1 kg mass weighs 2.20462 lbs. On the Moon (1/6 Earth's gravity), the same 1 kg mass weighs only 0.37 lbs. Your mass doesn't change on the Moon, but your weight does. For everyday Earth-bound use, we treat kg and lbs interchangeably as "weight," but scientists and engineers distinguish them. This calculator converts Earth-surface weights — lunar astronauts need different calculations!

How accurate are bathroom scales in different units?

Most digital scales measure in kg internally, then convert to lbs for display (or vice versa). Accuracy depends on the scale's sensors, typically ±0.2-0.5 lbs or ±0.1-0.2 kg. The unit conversion itself is exact — the error comes from the scale's measurement, not the math. For consistent tracking, use the same scale, same unit, same time of day (morning is most consistent), same surface (hard floor, not carpet). Switching between kg and lbs on the same scale is fine — the conversion is precise.

Why do some countries use stones for body weight?

The stone (14 lbs) dates to medieval England, where it was used for trading wool, meat, and grain. Different "stones" existed for different commodities (8 lbs for honey, 16 lbs for cheese). The 14-lb stone became standard for body weight in the 19th century. The UK officially adopted metric in 1965 but allowed stones for body weight as an "imperial exception" due to public attachment. British people born before ~1995 think naturally in stones; younger Brits use kg. Americans never adopted stones, using lbs exclusively. For international communication, kg is the universal standard.
