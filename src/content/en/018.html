What is the Mortar Plaster/Rendering Calculator?

The Mortar Plaster/Rendering Calculator determines the volume of mortar required for plastering or rendering walls (revestimiento de mortero). Construction teams throughout Spain use this tool when estimating materials for enlucidos (interior plaster) and revocos (exterior rendering) that protect masonry surfaces and provide smooth finishes for painting or decoration.

You may also find the Sprayed Plaster Calculator, Cement Mortar Calculator, and Filler and Putty Calculator useful.

Plastering applications vary by location and purpose. Interior walls (enlucido) typically receive 10-15 mm gypsum plaster over mortar base coat. Exterior walls (revoco) require 15-25 mm cement:sand rendering with waterproofer for weather resistance. Rough or uneven walls may require thicker applications to achieve level surfaces.

This calculator processes three inputs: length, height, and plaster thickness. Results include total mortar volume, which converts to cement and sand quantities based on mix ratio. Accurate estimation prevents material shortages during finishing phases and ensures proper color consistency across large wall areas.

Formula Explained with Actual Calculations

Plaster Area (m²) = Length (m) × Height (m)

Mortar Volume (m³) = Area (m²) × Thickness (m)

The formula calculates plaster volume as thin rectangular slab. Plaster thickness typically 10-15 mm for interior walls (enlucido), 15-25 mm for exterior rendering (revoco). For rough or uneven walls, measure at multiple points and use average thickness for calculations.

Input Variables:

  • Length (L): Horizontal wall surface length in meters. Example: 4.0 m for bedroom wall.
  • Height (H): Vertical wall surface height in meters. Example: 2.70 m standard ceiling.
  • Thickness (T): Plaster layer thickness in meters. Example: 0.015 m (15 mm) interior, 0.020 m (20 mm) exterior.

Standard Plaster Specifications:

  • Interior base coat (enlucido): 10-15 mm, 1:4 cement:sand
  • Interior finish coat (yeso): 2-5 mm, pre-mixed gypsum
  • Exterior base coat (revoco): 12-15 mm, 1:4 cement:sand
  • Exterior finish coat: 5-8 mm, 1:3 cement:sand with waterproofer
  • Waterproof rendering: 20-25 mm, 1:3 with integral waterproofing

Step-by-Step Calculation Process

Step 1: Measure wall dimensions from architectural plans or on-site. Drawing might specify: "Paramento P-1: L=4.0 m, H=2.70 m"

Step 2: Calculate gross area: 4.0 m × 2.70 m = 10.80 m².

Step 3: Deduct openings (windows, doors). For 1 window (1.20×1.50 m): 1.20 × 1.50 = 1.80 m².

Step 4: Net area: 10.80 − 1.80 = 9.00 m².

Step 5: Calculate mortar volume: 9.00 m² × 0.015 m = 0.135 m³.

Step 6: Convert to cement and sand (1:4 ratio): cement = 0.135 / 5 = 0.027 m³ = 1 bag (25 kg), sand = 0.135 × 4/5 = 0.108 m³.

Common Mistakes

  • Not deducting openings from area: Windows, doors, and other openings reduce plaster area. Always deduct opening areas from gross wall area before calculating mortar volume. For 72 m² wall with 9.30 m² openings: deduct 13% from material quantities. Failing to deduct results in 10-20% material over-ordering and wasted money.
  • Using wrong thickness for wall condition: Plaster thickness varies depending on wall surface. Smooth blockwork: 10-12 mm sufficient. Rough brickwork: 15-20 mm needed. Irregular stone: 25-30 mm may be required. Measure actual surface irregularity at multiple points using straightedge. Using uniform 15 mm for all walls creates shortages on rough surfaces and waste on smooth surfaces.
  • Forgetting finish coat in two-coat systems: Many plastering systems use base coat and finish coat with different mix ratios. Calculate each coat separately as they use different materials and quantities. Base coat (12-15 mm) provides bulk thickness. Finish coat (5-8 mm) provides final texture and weather resistance. Omitting finish coat from estimate creates mid-job material shortage.
  • Not accounting for waste and overlap: Plastering generates significant waste from drop, over-application, and cleanup. Add 10-15% to calculated volume for waste. For exterior rendering, scaffolding overlap at edges adds 5-10% additional area. For 100 m² calculated area: order materials for 110-115 m² to ensure adequate coverage without mid-job stops.

Pro Tips from Plastering Specialists

Tip 1: Apply exterior rendering in two coats for best results. Base coat (12-15 mm) fills surface irregularities and provides bulk thickness. Scratch base coat with comb or nail float while still plastic to provide mechanical key for finish coat. Allow base coat to cure minimum 7 days before applying finish coat. Finish coat (5-8 mm) provides final texture and weather resistance. Two-coat application prevents cracking and ensures uniform appearance.

Tip 2: Apply render in shaded conditions whenever possible. Direct sunlight causes rapid drying and surface cracking. In summer, work on north and east faces in morning, south and west faces in late afternoon. Use shade cloth to protect fresh render from direct sun. Rapid drying reduces bond strength and causes plastic shrinkage cracks. In hot weather (>30°C), mist walls with water before applying render to reduce suction.

Tip 3: Dampen substrate before applying plaster to prevent rapid water absorption from mortar. Dry masonry absorbs mixing water, preventing proper cement hydration and reducing bond strength. Spray walls with water 1-2 hours before plastering—surface should be damp but not dripping. In hot weather, dampen twice: once 2 hours before, once 15 minutes before application. Proper substrate preparation prevents plaster delamination.

Tip 4: Use correct trowel technique for each coat. Base coat: apply with steel trowel, pressing firmly to ensure good bond. Fill all voids and irregularities. Finish coat: apply with plastic or wooden float for textured finishes, steel trowel for smooth finishes. Work in manageable sections (1-2 m²), completing each section before moving to next. Blend edges of adjacent sections while still wet to avoid visible joints.

Tip 5: Cure plaster properly for maximum durability. Keep plastered surfaces moist for 3-7 days by misting 2-3 times daily. In hot weather, cover with damp burlap or plastic sheeting. Uncured plaster develops surface crazing and reduced strength. For exterior rendering, curing critical for weather resistance. Begin curing within 2-4 hours of application (after initial set). Continue curing throughout hot or windy conditions.

Frequently Asked Questions

Interior plastering (enlucido): base coat 1:4 cement:sand ratio, finish coat 1:3 ratio or pre-mixed gypsum plaster. Exterior rendering (revoco): base coat 1:4 ratio, finish coat 1:3 ratio with waterproofer admixture. For gypsum plaster (yeso), use pre-mixed products applied at 2-5 mm thickness over mortar base coat. Always use clean, well-graded sand free from clay and organic matter. Add plasticizer or lime for improved workability.

For 1:4 cement:sand mix: cement volume = total volume / 5, sand volume = total volume × 4/5. For 1.0 m³ of 1:4 mortar: cement = 0.20 m³ = 8 bags of 25 kg (1 bag = 0.025 m³), sand = 0.80 m³. For 1:3 mix: cement = total volume / 4, sand = total volume × 3/4. Add 10-15% to sand volume to account for bulking (sand expands when damp). Always order slightly more cement than calculated for adjustments.

For cement:sand mortar: wait minimum 24-48 hours between base coat and finish coat. Keep base coat moist (curing) during waiting period. For gypsum plaster over mortar base coat: wait until base coat has fully cured (7 days minimum). Substrate should be damp but not wet when applying each coat. In hot weather, reduce waiting time and apply more frequently to prevent base coat from drying out. Test by pressing thumb—coat should be firm but leave slight impression.

Plastering directly over painted walls not recommended—paint creates smooth, non-porous surface preventing proper adhesion. If plastering over paint is necessary: roughen surface with sandpaper or wire brush, apply bonding agent (puente de adhesión), then apply plaster. For best results, remove paint entirely using scraper, chemical stripper, or sandblasting before plastering. Test adhesion by applying small patch and allowing to cure 24 hours before proceeding with full wall.
