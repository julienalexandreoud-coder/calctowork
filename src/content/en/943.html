Date Difference Calculator: days, weeks and months

This calculator determines the exact difference between two dates, expressing it in days, weeks, months and years, useful for planning projects, computing deadlines and measuring time intervals.

How the difference is calculated

The calculator computes:

  • Total days: exact number of days between the two dates.
  • Weeks: total days divided by 7.
  • Months and years: breakdown into years, months and remaining days.
  • Business days: excluding weekends (optional).

Example 1: difference between two dates

Problem: Start date: January 1, 2025. End date: June 30, 2025.

  1. Total days:
    • 31 (Jan) + 28 (Feb) + 31 (Mar) + 30 (Apr) + 31 (May) + 30 (Jun) − 1 = 180 days.
  2. Weeks:
    • 180 / 7 ≈ 25.7 weeks.
  3. Months:
    • Approximately 5 months and 29 days.

Answer: 180 days, 25 weeks and 5 days, approximately 6 months.

Example 2: business days

Problem: Project from March 1 to May 31, 2025. How many business days?

  1. Total days:
    • 92 days (March 31 + April 30 + May 31).
  2. Business days (approximate):
    • 92 − weekends ≈ 66 business days.

Answer: 92 total days, approximately 66 business days.

Common uses of the date calculator

  • Computing delivery deadlines for projects and tasks.
  • Determining days remaining for important events.
  • Calculating contract and subscription durations.
  • Planning vacations and counting leave days.
  • Computing daily interest on financial products.
  • Measuring employment tenure or time of service.

Common mistakes in date calculations

  • Not considering that February has 28 or 29 days depending on the year.
  • Incorrectly including or excluding the start or end date.
  • Not distinguishing between calendar days and business days.
  • Forgetting that months have different numbers of days.
