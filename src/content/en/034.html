What is the Water Tank Size Calculator?

The water tank size calculator determines the required water storage capacity (depósito de agua) based on number of users, daily consumption per person, and desired days of autonomy. It calculates total tank volume in litres and cubic metres, helping designers size water storage for residential, commercial, and emergency backup systems.

You may also find the Water Pressure Calculator, Electric Water Heater Calculator, and Pool Filtration Calculator useful.

In Spanish construction, water storage follows CTE DB-HS 4 (Suministro de agua) which specifies minimum 200 litres per person per day for residential buildings, with 1-2 days of autonomy for elevated tanks. The calculator helps contractors working on residential buildings (edificios residenciales), rural properties (fincas rurales), commercial buildings, and emergency water systems determine appropriate tank sizes from 500 L to 50,000+ L.

Formula Explained

V_total = N × C × D   |   V_recomendado = V_total × 1.20

Where N is the number of people (usuarios), C is consumption per person per day in litres (L/p/día), D is days of autonomy (días de autonomía), and V_total is the required tank volume in litres.

Standard consumption rates: Residential 150-200 L/p/día, Commercial offices 50-80 L/p/día, Hotels 250-400 L/p/día, Hospitals 400-600 L/p/día. Add 20% safety margin for unexpected demand and tank sediment space.

Step-by-Step Calculation Process

  1. Count number of users: For residential, count occupants (typically 2 persons per bedroom + 1). For commercial, count employees plus visitors. For hotels, count beds or rooms × 2 occupants.
  2. Select consumption rate: Choose appropriate consumption based on building type. Spanish average is 150 L/p/día for water-efficient homes, 200 L/p/día for standard, 250+ L/p/día for luxury with pools and gardens.
  3. Determine autonomy days: Urban areas with reliable supply: 1 day autonomy. Suburban areas: 1-2 days. Rural areas with well or delivery: 3-7 days. Emergency backup: 7-14 days minimum.
  4. Calculate base volume: Multiply users × consumption × days. For 4 people, 200 L/d, 2 days: 4 × 200 × 2 = 1,600 litres.
  5. Add safety margin: Multiply by 1.20 for 20% safety margin. This accounts for higher-than-expected usage, tank sediment space, and future occupancy increases. 1,600 × 1.20 = 1,920 litres.
  6. Select standard tank size: Round up to nearest standard tank size. Common sizes: 500 L, 750 L, 1,000 L, 1,500 L, 2,000 L, 3,000 L, 5,000 L, 10,000 L. For 1,920 L, select 2,000 L or 3,000 L tank.

Real Examples with Specific Numbers

Example 1: Single-family home with roof tank

A 4-bedroom house is occupied by 5 people (2 adults, 3 children). Municipal supply is reliable but occasional interruptions occur. Standard consumption with garden irrigation.

Calculation: Users = 5 people. Consumption = 200 L/p/d (includes garden). Autonomy = 2 days. Base volume = 5 × 200 × 2 = 2,000 litres. With 20% margin: 2,000 × 1.20 = 2,400 litres.

Recommendation: Install 2,500-3,000 L polyethylene tank (depósito de polietileno). Dimensions approximately 1.5 m diameter × 1.8 m height. Cost: €400-600 for tank, €200-300 for installation and plumbing. Position on reinforced roof structure or ground-level pump house.

Example 2: Rural property with well water

A rural farmhouse (casa rural) has 8 bedrooms for tourism, accommodating up to 16 guests. Water comes from a well with limited yield. High consumption expected with pools and laundry.

Calculation: Users = 16 guests + 2 staff = 18 people. Consumption = 300 L/p/d (tourism with pool). Autonomy = 5 days (well recovery time). Base volume = 18 × 300 × 5 = 27,000 litres. With 20% margin: 27,000 × 1.20 = 32,400 litres.

Recommendation: Install 35,000-40,000 L underground or semi-buried tank. Underground installation prevents algae growth and freezing. Cost: €4,000-6,000 for tank, €3,000-5,000 for excavation and installation. Add UV filtration for potable use.

Example 3: Small office building

An office building has 25 employees plus an estimated 10 visitors daily. Water use is limited to restrooms and kitchenette. Municipal supply is reliable.

Calculation: Users = 35 people (employees + visitors). Consumption = 60 L/p/d (office use only). Autonomy = 1 day (reliable supply). Base volume = 35 × 60 × 1 = 2,100 litres. With 20% margin: 2,100 × 1.20 = 2,520 litres.

Recommendation: Install 3,000 L tank in building basement or service room. Compact vertical tank (1.2 m diameter × 2.5 m height) fits in tight spaces. Cost: €500-700 for tank, €300-400 installation. Tank provides buffer for peak morning demand and municipal supply interruptions.

Example 4: Emergency water storage for apartment building

A 20-unit apartment building (average 3 people per unit = 60 residents) wants emergency water storage for disaster preparedness. Goal is 7 days autonomy with minimal consumption.

Calculation: Users = 60 people. Consumption = 50 L/p/d (emergency minimum - drinking, sanitation only). Autonomy = 7 days. Base volume = 60 × 50 × 7 = 21,000 litres. With 20% margin: 21,000 × 1.20 = 25,200 litres.

Recommendation: Install multiple tanks totaling 25,000-30,000 L. Options: 3× 10,000 L tanks (€2,400 total) or 5× 5,000 L tanks (€2,000 total). Multiple smaller tanks are easier to install and maintain. Add hand pump or 12V DC pump for emergency water extraction during power outages.

Example 5: Hotel with 50 rooms

A 3-star hotel has 50 rooms, average occupancy 1.8 guests per room = 90 guests, plus 20 staff. Full amenities including restaurant, pool, and laundry.

Calculation: Users = 90 guests + 20 staff = 110 people. Consumption = 350 L/p/d (hotel with amenities). Autonomy = 2 days (municipal backup). Base volume = 110 × 350 × 2 = 77,000 litres. With 20% margin: 77,000 × 1.20 = 92,400 litres.

Recommendation: Install 100,000 L water storage system. Best configuration: 2× 50,000 L tanks (€8,000-10,000 each) or 4× 25,000 L tanks. Underground installation preferred for temperature stability and aesthetics. Add commercial water treatment (filtration, UV, chlorination) for guest safety. Total system cost: €25,000-35,000 installed.

Common Mistakes

  • Underestimating consumption per person: Spanish average of 150 L/p/d is for water-conscious households with efficient fixtures. Actual consumption with standard fixtures, baths (not showers), and garden irrigation easily reaches 250-300 L/p/d. Using 100 L/p/d for calculations results in 50-60% undersized tanks.
  • Not accounting for autonomy needs: Urban areas with reliable municipal supply may only need 1 day autonomy. Rural areas with wells need 3-7 days to allow well recovery. Properties dependent on water delivery trucks need 7-14 days. Insufficient autonomy means running out of water during supply interruptions.
  • Forgetting safety margin: Tanks should never be sized exactly to calculated demand. The 20% margin accounts for: sediment accumulation at tank bottom (5-10%), unexpected guests, higher summer consumption, and future occupancy increases. Tanks without margin operate at maximum capacity constantly, reducing lifespan.
  • Ignoring structural requirements: Water weighs 1,000 kg per m³. A 5,000 L tank weighs 5 tonnes when full. Roof installations require structural engineering verification. Ground-level tanks need level, compacted base. Underground tanks require proper bedding and anchoring to prevent flotation in high water table.

Pro Tips from Specialists

  1. Install tanks in series for large capacities: For capacities over 10,000 L, install multiple tanks in series (en serie) rather than single large tank. Benefits: easier installation through standard doors, redundancy if one tank fails, ability to isolate for cleaning without complete shutdown, and often lower total cost.
  2. Provide tank access for cleaning: Tanks require annual inspection and cleaning. Ensure access hatch (tapa de registro) is reachable with 600 mm minimum opening. Install overflow pipe (desbordamiento) directed to visible location for leak detection. Add drain valve (válvula de vaciado) at lowest point for complete emptying.
  3. Protect tanks from sunlight and freezing: UV radiation degrades polyethylene tanks and promotes algae growth. Use UV-stabilised tanks or paint exterior with reflective coating. In freezing climates, bury tanks below frost line (60-80 cm in Spain), insulate exposed tanks, or install in heated spaces. Frozen tanks crack and fail.
  4. Install first-flush diverter for rainwater: If collecting rainwater (agua de lluvia), install first-flush diverter (separador de primeras aguas) that discards first 20-40 L of each rainfall, removing roof debris and bird droppings. This dramatically improves water quality and reduces tank cleaning frequency. Costs €50-100, essential for potable rainwater systems.
  5. Add water level indicator: Install sight glass (visor de nivel), float gauge, or electronic level sensor to monitor tank contents. Running a tank completely dry damages pumps and allows sediment to enter pipes. Electronic sensors with remote display (€80-150) allow monitoring from inside the building.

Frequently Asked Questions

Spanish average consumption varies by lifestyle: Water-efficient homes with efficient fixtures: 100-150 L/p/d. Standard homes: 150-200 L/p/d. Homes with pools and gardens: 250-400 L/p/d. Hotels: 250-400 L/p/d. Hospitals: 400-600 L/p/d. Emergency minimum (drinking and sanitation only): 30-50 L/p/d. CTE DB-HS 4 uses 200 L/p/d for residential design calculations.

Polyethylene (polietileno) is most common - lightweight, corrosion-resistant, €0.40-0.80/L. Fibreglass (fibra de vidrio) is durable for large tanks, €0.60-1.20/L. Concrete (hormigón) for underground or architectural integration, €0.80-1.50/L. Steel with epoxy lining for very large tanks, €1.00-2.00/L. For most residential applications, polyethylene offers best value.

Underground (enterrado) advantages: temperature stability, no freezing, no UV degradation, out of sight. Disadvantages: higher installation cost, difficult access for maintenance, flotation risk in high water table. Above ground (aéreo) advantages: lower cost, easy access, visible leaks. Disadvantages: temperature variation, UV exposure, space requirement. For Spain, underground is preferred for climate reasons.

Residential tanks should be inspected annually and cleaned every 2-3 years. Commercial and public buildings require annual cleaning per health regulations. Signs tank needs cleaning: visible sediment, odour, discoloured water, algae growth. Cleaning involves: drain completely, scrub interior with diluted bleach (1:100), rinse thoroughly, refill. Costs €150-300 for professional cleaning.

Only if tank is certified for potable water (agua potable) and water is treated. Look for tanks marked "aptas para agua potable" per UNE-EN 12573. Add water treatment: sediment filter (€50-100), UV steriliser (€200-400), or chlorination system (€150-300). Test water annually for bacteria and contaminants. Rainwater requires more extensive treatment than municipal backup.
