What Is a Solar Panel Installation Calculator?

A solar panel installation calculator determines the number of panels, total system capacity (kWp), and expected energy production based on daily consumption, peak sun hours, and panel power rating. For a household consuming 15 kWh daily in a location with 4.5 peak sun hours, using 400 W panels, the calculator specifies a 3.33 kWp system requiring 9 panels (3,600 W installed).

You may also find the Battery Storage Calculator, Monthly Electricity Consumption Calculator, and Electrical Panel Calculator useful.

Accurate sizing prevents undersized systems that fail to meet energy needs (requiring grid supplementation costing €800-€1,500 annually) or oversized systems wasting €2,000-€5,000 on unused capacity. A properly sized 5 kWp system in Southern Europe produces 7,500-8,500 kWh annually, covering 85-95% of typical household consumption.

The calculation uses daily energy consumption (kWh), local peak sun hours (PSH), panel wattage, system efficiency losses (15-25%), and available roof area to determine optimal system configuration.

Solar Panel Formulas With Calculations

Daily Energy Needed (kWh) = Consumption × Autonomy Factor

Example: 15 kWh/day × 1.0 (grid-tied) = 15 kWh/day

15 kWh/day × 1.5 (off-grid with battery) = 22.5 kWh/day

Required System Size (kWp) = Daily Energy ÷ Peak Sun Hours

15 kWh ÷ 4.5 PSH = 3.33 kWp

Number of Panels = Required kWp × 1,000 ÷ Panel Wattage

3.33 kWp × 1,000 ÷ 400 W = 8.33 → 9 panels

Installed Capacity (kWp) = Panels × Panel Wattage ÷ 1,000

9 × 400 W ÷ 1,000 = 3.6 kWp

Annual Production (kWh) = Installed kWp × PSH × 365 × System Efficiency

3.6 kWp × 4.5 h × 365 × 0.80 (efficiency) = 4,730 kWh/year

Roof Area Required = Panels × Panel Area

Standard 400 W panel: 1.7 m × 1.1 m = 1.87 m²

9 panels × 1.87 m² = 16.8 m² (plus 20% spacing = 20.2 m² total)

6 Steps To Calculate Solar Panel Requirements

  1. Determine daily energy consumption from electricity bills: Annual consumption ÷ 365 = daily kWh. Example: 5,475 kWh/year ÷ 365 = 15 kWh/day. For new construction, estimate 3-5 kWh/day per person. Four-person household: 12-20 kWh/day. Include future loads: EV charging (8-12 kWh/day), heat pump (5-8 kWh/day), pool pump (2-4 kWh/day).
  2. Find peak sun hours for your location: PSH represents equivalent hours of 1,000 W/m² solar irradiance. Southern Spain: 5.0-5.5 PSH. Northern Germany: 2.5-3.0 PSH. Central Europe average: 3.5-4.0 PSH. Use PVGIS (European Commission) or NASA SSE database for precise coordinates. Winter PSH is 40-60% lower than summer—design for annual average or specify battery backup for winter autonomy.
  3. Calculate required system size before losses: Daily kWh ÷ PSH = kWp needed. For 15 kWh/day and 4.5 PSH: 3.33 kWp. This is DC rating before inverter and wiring losses. Never size based on AC output—panel ratings are DC standard test conditions (STC).
  4. Apply system efficiency derating factors: Inverter efficiency: 95-98%. Wiring losses: 2-3%. Temperature derating: 10-15% (panels lose 0.4-0.5% per °C above 25°C). Soiling (dust): 2-5%. Mismatch losses: 2-3%. Combined efficiency: 75-85%. Use 80% for conservative estimates. Adjusted production: 3.33 kWp × 4.5 PSH × 365 × 0.80 = 4,388 kWh/year.

  5. Select panel count and verify roof space: Divide required kWp by panel wattage. 3.33 kWp ÷ 0.400 kW = 8.33 → 9 panels. Check roof area: 9 panels × 1.87 m² = 16.8 m². Add 20% for spacing (maintenance access, rail mounting): 20.2 m² total. Verify roof orientation: south-facing ideal, east/west acceptable with 15-20% production penalty. Roof pitch 30-35° optimal for annual production.
  6. Size inverter with 10-20% headroom: Inverter AC rating should be 80-90% of DC panel capacity (oversizing panels maximizes production in low light). For 3.6 kWp DC, use 3.0-3.3 kW AC inverter. String inverters (€1,200-€2,000) for simple roofs. Microinverters (€180-€250 per panel) for shaded or complex roofs. Hybrid inverters (€2,500-€4,000) for future battery addition.

4 Practical Examples

Example 1 — Small Household (2 persons, 8 kWh/day): Location: Valencia, Spain (4.8 PSH). Required: 8 ÷ 4.8 = 1.67 kWp. Panels: 1,670 ÷ 400 = 4.2 → 5 panels. Installed: 2.0 kWp. Annual production: 2.0 × 4.8 × 365 × 0.80 = 2,803 kWh. Covers 95% of 8 kWh/day (2,920 kWh/year) consumption. Roof area: 5 × 1.87 × 1.2 = 11.2 m². Inverter: 1.8 kW single-phase. System cost: €3,200-€4,200 installed. Payback: 6-8 years at €0.28/kWh.

Example 2 — Family Home (4 persons, 18 kWh/day with EV): Location: Lyon, France (3.6 PSH). Base consumption 12 kWh + EV 6 kWh = 18 kWh/day. Required: 18 ÷ 3.6 = 5.0 kWp. Panels: 5,000 ÷ 420 = 11.9 → 12 panels. Installed: 5.04 kWp. Annual: 5.04 × 3.6 × 365 × 0.80 = 5,297 kWh. Covers 81% of 6,570 kWh/year. Roof: 12 × 1.95 × 1.2 = 28.1 m². Inverter: 4.5 kW single-phase or 5.0 kW three-phase. System cost: €7,500-€9,500. Payback: 7-9 years.

Example 3 — Off-Grid Cabin (6 kWh/day, winter autonomy): Location: Austrian Alps (2.8 PSH summer, 1.2 PSH winter). Design for winter: 6 ÷ 1.2 = 5.0 kWp minimum. Panels: 5,000 ÷ 400 = 12.5 → 13 panels. Battery: 6 kWh × 3 days autonomy ÷ 0.85 DoD = 21.2 kWh usable. Lithium battery 25 kWh (€8,000-€12,000). Inverter: 5 kW hybrid with generator input. Generator backup required for extended cloudy periods. Total system: €25,000-€35,000. Grid extension alternative: €40,000-€80,000 for 2 km distance.

Example 4 — Commercial Warehouse (150 kWh/day): Location: Milan, Italy (3.9 PSH). Required: 150 ÷ 3.9 = 38.5 kWp. Panels: 38,500 ÷ 550 (commercial panels) = 70 panels. Installed: 38.5 kWp. Annual: 38.5 × 3.9 × 365 × 0.82 = 44,850 kWh. Covers 82% of 54,750 kWh/year consumption. Roof: 70 × 2.2 m² × 1.15 spacing = 177 m². Inverter: 30 kW three-phase string inverter. Mounting: flat roof ballasted system (no roof penetration). System cost: €42,000-€55,000. Payback: 5-7 years with commercial electricity at €0.35/kWh.

4 Costly Mistakes To Avoid

  • Ignoring shading from chimneys, trees, and adjacent buildings: A single panel shaded 20% can reduce string output by 60-80% without optimizers. Shading analysis with SunEye or Solmetric tools (€2,500-€4,000) identifies problem areas. Professional installers use drone thermography (€350-€600) to detect hot spots. Shading losses: 15-40% annual production. Solutions: microinverters (€200-€250 per panel), DC optimizers (€80-€120 per panel), or relocate panels to unshaded areas.
  • Oversizing system beyond self-consumption without battery: Export tariffs (€0.05-€0.10/kWh) are 60-70% lower than import costs (€0.28-€0.35/kWh). A 10 kWp system producing 40% excess exports earns €150-€250/year versus €600-€900/year value if consumed. Optimal self-consumption: 70-85%. Systems over 8 kWp for typical households waste €2,000-€4,000 in unrealized savings. Add battery (€6,000-€10,000) for 50%+ excess, or right-size system to match daytime load.
  • Using mismatched panel orientations in same string: Panels facing different directions (east vs west) or with different tilt angles produce different voltages, reducing string efficiency by 15-25%. All panels in a string must have identical orientation, tilt, and preferably same model/age. Mixed orientations require separate MPPT inputs on inverter or microinverters. Correcting post-installation: €800-€1,500 for rewiring and additional mounting hardware.
  • Underestimating roof load capacity for older buildings: Solar panels add 15-20 kg/m² (panels + mounting + snow load). Pre-1980 roofs may not support this without reinforcement. Structural engineer assessment (€400-€800) required for buildings over 40 years old. Roof reinforcement: €2,000-€8,000 depending on extent. Installing without verification risks roof collapse under snow load (insurance denies claims for unapproved structural modifications).

5 Professional Tips

  • Oversize panels 20-30% relative to inverter: Panels rated at STC (1,000 W/m²) rarely produce nameplate wattage in real conditions (800-900 W/m² typical). A 5.0 kWp array with 4.0 kW inverter maximizes production during morning/evening/cloudy days. Inverter clipping losses: 2-4% annually. Increased low-light production: 12-18%. Net gain: 8-14% more energy for €400-€800 additional panel cost. Payback: 2-3 years.
  • Install monitoring system with production alerts: WiFi monitoring (included with most inverters) shows real-time production, consumption, and export. Set alerts for production drops >20% (indicates faults, shading, or inverter issues). Early detection prevents 60-80% production losses from going unnoticed for months. Apps: SolarEdge, Enphase, Fronius Solar.web, SMA Sunny Portal. Professional monitoring services: €15-€30/month for commercial systems.
  • Plan conduit runs during roof installation or re-roofing: Conduit from roof to electrical room costs €25-€45/m installed during construction. Core drilling through finished walls: €180-€350 per penetration. Surface-mounted conduit: €35-€55/m (less aesthetic). Coordinate solar rough-in with roofing contractor before tiles/sheets installed. Proper planning saves €600-€1,200 in electrical penetration costs.
  • Apply for grid connection approval before installation: Most EU countries require utility approval for systems over 800 W. Approval process: 2-8 weeks. Installing without approval: utility can disconnect, refuse net metering, and impose €1,000-€5,000 fines. Some regions have capacity limits—grid may be saturated, delaying connection 6-18 months. Feed-in tariff applications often require pre-approval documentation. Start process 3-4 months before planned installation.
  • Clean panels twice annually for maximum production: Dust, pollen, and bird droppings reduce output 8-15% in rural areas, 15-25% near agriculture or industry. Cleaning cost: €120-€200 for residential (professional service). DIY: soft brush, deionized water, early morning/evening only (thermal shock cracks hot panels). Annual cleaning schedule: March (after winter storms), September (after pollen season). Production gain: 10-18% annually. Payback: 6-12 months.
