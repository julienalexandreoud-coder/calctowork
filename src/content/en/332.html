Deciding how precise you need to be

For everyday calculations, the most important question isn't 'is this result exact?' but 'is it accurate enough for the decision I'm making?' A tip calculated to the cent is no more useful than one rounded to the nearest dollar. Percentage discounts matter when comparing two options; the exact decimal is irrelevant. Calibrate how much precision you actually need to the stakes of the decision — this prevents both under-thinking (rough guesses that mislead) and over-thinking (false precision that creates busy-work).

Why accurate calculation matters
