What is the Road Base/Pavement Calculator?

The road base calculator determines the volume of aggregate material required to construct the base and subbase layers of a road pavement structure. These layers sit between the subgrade and the surface course (aglomerado asfáltico), providing structural strength, load distribution, and drainage. Accurate calculation of base course volumes is essential for material ordering, cost estimation, and ensuring the pavement meets design specifications for traffic loading.

In Spanish road construction, pavement design follows PG-3 (Pliego de Prescripciones Técnicas Generales para Obras de Carreteras) and NLT (Normas de Ensayo de Laboratorio) standards. The calculator helps contractors working on urban roads (viales urbanos), industrial access roads (accesos industriales), and parking areas determine quantities for granular subbases (todo-uno), crushed stone bases (agregado triturado), and cement-treated bases (base tratada con cemento).

Formula Explained

V = L × W × E

Where V is the aggregate volume in cubic metres (m³), L is the road length in metres (m), W is the pavement width in metres (m), and E is the total thickness of the base layer(s) in metres (m). For multi-layer pavement structures, calculate each layer separately and sum the results.

The formula yields the compacted volume; multiply by the loose-to-compacted ratio (typically 1.20-1.30) to determine the delivery volume. To convert to tonnes, multiply by material density: granular subbase ≈ 2.10 t/m³, crushed stone base ≈ 2.20 t/m³.

Step-by-Step Calculation Process

  1. Measure pavement dimensions: Record total road length (L) and pavement width (W). Include full width from kerb to kerb (bordillo a bordillo). For roads with shoulders, calculate main carriageway and shoulders separately as they often have different layer thicknesses.
  2. Identify layer structure: Review pavement design drawings to determine number of layers and individual thicknesses. Typical Spanish urban road: 200 mm subbase (todo-uno 0/40) + 180 mm base (agregado 0/31.5) = 380 mm total.
  3. Calculate individual layer volumes: For each layer, multiply length × width × thickness. Use compacted thickness from design drawings, not loose thickness.
  4. Sum total volumes: Add all layer volumes together if ordering materials together, or keep separate if different materials are specified for each layer.
  5. Apply compaction factor: Multiply each compacted volume by 1.20-1.30 to determine loose delivery quantities. Well-graded aggregates use 1.20-1.22; uniform-graded materials may need 1.25-1.30.
  6. Convert to tonnes (optional):strong> Multiply compacted volume by material density for weight-based ordering. Subbase at 2.10 t/m³, crushed base at 2.20 t/m³, recycled aggregate at 2.00 t/m³.

Real Examples with Specific Numbers

Example 1: Single-layer road base for rural access road

A rural access road (camino de acceso) is 500 m long and 5.00 m wide. The pavement design specifies a single 250 mm thick layer of crushed aggregate base course (agregado triturado 0/40 mm) compacted to 98% MDD.

Calculation: V = 500 × 5.00 × 0.25 = 625.00 m³

The compacted volume is 625 m³ of aggregate base course. With a compaction factor of 1.25, the loose delivery volume is 625 × 1.25 = 781.25 m³, requiring approximately 78 truck loads of 10 m³ each. At €16/m³ delivered, material costs total €10,000.

Example 2: Multi-layer pavement for urban arterial road

An urban arterial road (vía de distribución) section is 150 m long and 7.00 m wide (two lanes). The pavement design calls for a 200 mm subbase (todo-uno 0/63 mm granular) plus a 180 mm base course (agregado triturado 0/31.5 mm).

Calculation: Subbase volume = 150 × 7.00 × 0.20 = 210.00 m³. Base course volume = 150 × 7.00 × 0.18 = 189.00 m³. Total = 210.00 + 189.00 = 399.00 m³.

Total aggregate requirement is 399 m³ (compacted). Each layer uses different aggregate gradations and must be ordered separately. Subbase needs 210 m³ of 0/63 mm material at €14/m³ (€2,940). Base course needs 189 m³ of 0/31.5 mm at €18/m³ (€3,402).

Example 3: Industrial yard with cement-treated base

A logistics yard (patio de logística) measures 100 m × 50 m. Design specifies 150 mm granular subbase (0/40 mm) plus 200 mm cement-treated base (BTC 3/22.5 with 3.5% cement).

Calculation: Subbase = 100 × 50 × 0.15 = 750.00 m³. BTC layer = 100 × 50 × 0.20 = 1,000.00 m³. Subbase mass = 750 × 2.10 = 1,575 tonnes. BTC soil mass = 1,000 × 2.20 = 2,200 tonnes. Cement = 2,200 × 0.035 = 77 tonnes.

Order 750 m³ subbase (€10,500), 1,000 m³ aggregate for BTC (€18,000), and 77 tonnes CEM II/B-S 32.5N cement at €95/tonne (€7,315). Total materials: €35,815 plus mixing and compaction labour.

Example 4: Residential street with recycled aggregate

A residential street (calle residencial) is 80 m long and 6.50 m wide. Sustainable construction requirements specify 100% recycled concrete aggregate (RCA) for the 220 mm subbase layer.

Calculation: V = 80 × 6.50 × 0.22 = 114.40 m³. With 1.22 compaction factor: 114.40 × 1.22 = 139.57 m³ loose. Mass = 114.40 × 2.00 t/m³ = 228.80 tonnes.

Recycled aggregate costs €12/tonne versus €16/tonne for virgin material, saving €915. Order 229 tonnes RCA (approximately 23 truck loads). RCA meets Spanish UNE 12620 specifications for subbase applications.

Example 5: Car park pavement with geogrid reinforcement

A commercial car park (aparcamiento) measures 60 m × 40 m. Poor subgrade conditions (CBR = 4%) require geogrid reinforcement at subbase/base interface. Pavement structure: 250 mm subbase + 200 mm base course.

Calculation: Subbase = 60 × 40 × 0.25 = 600.00 m³. Base = 60 × 40 × 0.20 = 480.00 m³. Geogrid area = 60 × 40 = 2,400 m². Add 10% overlap: 2,400 × 1.10 = 2,640 m² geogrid required.

Subbase costs €8,400 (600 m³ at €14/m³). Base course costs €8,640 (480 m³ at €18/m³). Geogrid reinforcement at €2.50/m² costs €6,600. Total: €23,640. Geogrid allows 20% thickness reduction, potentially saving €3,400 in aggregate costs.

Common Mistakes

  • Calculating total pavement thickness as single layer: Subbase and base course use different aggregate gradations and quality specifications. Combining them into one calculation leads to ordering wrong material types. A 0/63 mm subbase aggregate cannot substitute for 0/31.5 mm base course.
  • Forgetting different layers require separate orders: Subbase (todo-uno 0/40 or 0/63) costs €12-16/m³ while base course (agregado triturado 0/22 or 0/31.5) costs €16-22/m³. Ordering base-course material for subbase applications wastes €4-6/m³ unnecessarily.
  • Not including edge support width: Pavement width should include full construction width, not just travel lanes. For a 6 m carriageway with 0.50 m edge support on each side, use 7.00 m width in calculations. Missing this adds 17% more material that must be ordered mid-project.
  • Using loose volume instead of compacted volume: Design thicknesses specify compacted dimensions. Ordering based on loose volume without compaction factor results in 20-30% material shortages. Always multiply compacted volume by 1.20-1.30 for delivery quantities.

Pro Tips from Specialists

  1. Reference grading curves in specifications: Spanish PG-3 Article 512 defines subbase gradations (todo-uno 0/40, 0/63) and base course gradations (agregado 0/22, 0/31.5). Specify Los Angeles abrasion (LA < 35 for subbase, LA < 30 for base) and Micro-Deval (MDE < 25) values for durability.
  2. Consider bound bases for heavy traffic: For roads with >10% heavy goods vehicles, cement-treated base (BTC) or bitumen-treated base (BTM) provides greater stiffness. BTC uses 3-5% cement by weight. Though 40-60% more expensive than granular base, it doubles pavement life under heavy loading.
  3. Verify aggregate source certification: Spanish CE marking per UNE-EN 13242 is mandatory for road aggregates. Request factory production control (FPC) certificates and test reports for LA coefficient, MDE value, and particle size distribution. Non-certified materials risk rejection during quality control inspections.
  4. Plan delivery sequencing by layer: Subbase must be placed, compacted, and approved before base course delivery begins. Coordinate truck schedules to avoid site congestion. A 600 m³ subbase requires 60 truck deliveries - schedule 8-10 trucks per day over 6-7 days.
  5. Calculate stockpile space requirements: Aggregates stockpile at 35° angle of repose. For 500 m³ delivery, plan 15 m × 10 m × 3 m stockpile footprint. Ensure adequate laydown area before ordering. Urban sites often lack space, requiring just-in-time delivery coordination.

Frequently Asked Questions

For subbase layers, Spanish PG-3 specifies todo-uno 0/40 mm or 0/63 mm graded aggregate. For base courses, use agregado triturado 0/22 mm or 0/31.5 mm crushed stone. Heavily trafficked roads (tráfico pesado) require higher-quality aggregates with LA < 25 and MDE < 20. Light traffic roads (tráfico ligero) can use LA < 35 materials.

Subbase is the lower granular layer sitting directly on subgrade, using larger less expensive aggregate (0/63 mm) and providing drainage and initial load distribution. Base course is the upper structural layer using higher-quality well-graded crushed aggregate (0/31.5 mm) carrying majority of traffic load. Base course must meet stricter LA/MDE requirements and costs 25-40% more per m³.

Multiply compacted volume by material's compacted density. Typical densities: granular subbase ≈ 2.10 t/m³, crushed stone base ≈ 2.20 t/m³, recycled aggregate ≈ 2.00 t/m³, gravel natural ≈ 1.95 t/m³. Example: 625 m³ crushed base × 2.20 t/m³ = 1,375 tonnes. Always confirm density with supplier as it varies with aggregate type, grading, and moisture content.

Recycled concrete aggregate (RCA - árido reciclado de hormigón) and recycled asphalt pavement (RAP - fresado de asfalto) can be used for subbase and some base applications per Spanish UNE 12620. RCA is commonly used for subbase layers and light-traffic base courses. Check PG-3 Article 512 for permitted recycled content percentages (typically 25-100% for subbase, 0-30% for base course).

PG-3 requires 98% of Modified Proctor density (UNE 103500) for base courses and 95-98% for subbase depending on road category. Achieve this with vibratory rollers (10-12 tonnes) making 6-8 passes per 200 mm lift. Test density every 500 m² using nuclear gauge or sand replacement method. Water content must be within ±2% of optimum for proper compaction.
