Hourly Rate to Annual Salary Calculator

This calculator converts an hourly rate into its annual salary equivalent, helping you project your yearly income based on your hourly rate and weekly work hours.

You may also find the Salary to Hourly Calculator, Mortgage Calculator, and Savings Calculator useful.

Conversion formula

The formula is:

Annual salary = hourly_rate × hours_per_week × 52

52 weeks per year are assumed. You can adjust weekly hours according to your work schedule.

Example 1: standard rate

Problem: Rate of $30/hour with a 40-hour workweek.

  1. Calculation:
    • $30 × 40 × 52 = $62,400/year.

Answer: Annual salary of $62,400.

Example 2: freelance rate

Problem: Rate of $50/hour working 30 hours per week.

  1. Calculation:
    • $50 × 30 × 52 = $78,000/year.

Answer: Annual salary of $78,000.

Common uses of this calculator

  • Projecting annual income from an hourly rate.
  • Comparing freelance work with salaried employment.
  • Negotiating hourly rates based on target annual salaries.
  • Planning annual budgets for independent workers.
  • Evaluating the impact of reducing or increasing work hours.
  • Estimating income for credit or mortgage applications.

Common mistakes in conversion

  • Not considering weeks not worked (vacation, illness).
  • Forgetting that freelancers pay their own taxes and insurance.
  • Not including the value of benefits that salaried employment would provide.
  • Assuming all weekly hours are billable.

Pro tip

If you are freelance, deduct at least 25-30% for taxes and social security, and consider that not all your hours are billable (administration, marketing, training). Your effective hourly rate will be lower.
