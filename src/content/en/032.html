What is the Copper and PEX Pipe Calculator?

The copper and PEX pipe calculator determines the quantities of water supply pipes, fittings, and accessories required for residential and commercial plumbing installations. It calculates pipe lengths, elbows, tees, valves, and mounting hardware based on total run length and number of supply points, enabling accurate material ordering for potable water systems.

You may also find the PVC Drainage Pipe Calculator, Underfloor Heating Calculator, and Water Supply Connection Calculator useful.

In Spanish construction, water supply installations follow CTE DB-HS 4 (Suministro de agua) and UNE-EN 1057 for copper tubes or UNE-EN 1587 for PEX systems. The calculator helps contractors working on residential plumbing (fontanería residencial), commercial buildings, and renovation projects determine copper (cobre) or cross-linked polyethylene (PEX) pipe quantities in 12mm, 14mm, 16mm, 18mm, and 22mm diameters.

Formula Explained

N° tubos = Longitud total / Longitud por tubo   |   N° accesorios = N° puntos × Factor por tipo

Where Longitud total is the total pipe run in metres (m), Longitud por tubo is the standard pipe length (3 m for copper, 50-100 m coils for PEX), and N° accesorios is estimated based on number of supply points (puntos de suministro).

Standard fitting estimation per supply point: 2 elbows, 1 tee, 1 valve. For copper: add solder (estaño) and flux (fundente). For PEX: add compression fittings (racords) or crimp rings (anillas).

Step-by-Step Calculation Process

  1. Map supply point locations: Count all fixtures: sinks (lavabos), showers (duchas), toilets (inodoros), washing machines (lavadoras), dishwashers (lavavajillas), water heater (termo). Each requires hot and/or cold supply.
  2. Measure pipe runs: Calculate distance from water meter/manifestation to each fixture. Include vertical runs (risers) and horizontal runs. Main supply lines use larger diameter (18-22mm), branch lines use smaller (12-16mm).
  3. Select pipe material: Copper (ácero cobre) is traditional, durable, €8-15/m. PEX is flexible, faster to install, €2-4/m. Multilayer (multicapa) combines benefits, €4-7/m. Choose based on budget and installation requirements.
  4. Calculate pipe quantities: For copper: divide total length by 3 m, round up. For PEX: order single coil of required length. Add 10% waste factor for cutting and errors.
  5. Count fittings by type: Elbows (codos) 90° and 45° for direction changes. Tees (tes) for branch connections. Valves (válvulas) for isolation. Reduce (reducciones) for diameter changes.
  6. Add accessories: Include pipe clips (abrazaderas) every 0.8-1 m for copper, every 0.5-0.6 m for PEX. Add insulation (aislamiento) for hot water pipes. Include solder, flux, or crimp tools as needed.

Common Mistakes

  • Undersizing main supply pipes: Using 14mm for main supply when 18-22mm is required causes pressure drop when multiple fixtures operate. Main supply from meter should be 22mm minimum for houses, 28mm for apartments with 3+ bathrooms. Branch lines to individual fixtures can be 14-16mm.
  • Missing isolation valves: Every fixture should have individual isolation valves (válvulas de corte) for maintenance without shutting off entire system. Install valves at each hot/cold connection. Costs €4-8 per valve but saves €150+ plumber call-out for minor repairs.
  • Insufficient pipe support spacing: Copper pipes need clips every 0.8-1 m horizontally, 1.5-2 m vertically. PEX needs clips every 0.5-0.6 m. Wider spacing causes pipe sag, noise (golpe de ariete), and joint stress. Use appropriate clip type for pipe material.
  • Forgetting hot water insulation: Uninsulated hot water pipes lose 5-10°C per metre, wasting energy and increasing wait time for hot water. Insulate all hot water pipes with 13mm minimum foam sleeves. Costs €1-2/m but saves €50-100/year in energy costs.

Pro Tips from Specialists

  1. Use manifold system for large installations: Traditional trunk-and-branch systems have pressure fluctuations when multiple fixtures operate. Manifold (distribuidor) systems run individual pipes from central manifold to each fixture, maintaining consistent pressure. Slightly more pipe (15-20%) but eliminates pressure drops and allows individual fixture isolation.
  2. Pressure test before concealing pipes: Always pressure test new installations at 1.5× working pressure (6 bar for 4 bar systems) for minimum 24 hours before concealing pipes. Use test pump (bomba de prueba) and monitor gauge for drops. Small leaks are easy to fix before walls close; catastrophic after.
  3. Install drain valves at low points: At the lowest point of the system, install a drain valve (válvula de vaciado) for system draining during maintenance or freeze protection. In unheated buildings, ability to completely drain pipes prevents freeze damage. Costs €15 but prevents €5,000+ freeze damage claims.
  4. Label manifold outlets clearly: With manifold systems, label each outlet with destination (e.g., "Baño 1 - Lavabo", "Cocina - Fregadero"). Use permanent marker or labels. During emergencies or maintenance, knowing which valve controls which fixture saves critical time.
  5. Use dielectric unions between dissimilar metals: When connecting copper to steel (galvanised pipes, water heater nipples), install dielectric unions (uniones dieléctricas) to prevent galvanic corrosion. Direct copper-to-steel connections corrode within 5-10 years. Dielectric unions cost €8-15 and last 30+ years.

Frequently Asked Questions

Copper (cobre) is traditional, durable (50+ years), handles high temperatures, but expensive (€8-15/m) and requires soldering. PEX is flexible, fast to install, freeze-resistant, cheap (€2-4/m), but requires special fittings and can't be used outdoors. Multilayer (multicapa) combines benefits: flexible like PEX, rigid like copper, oxygen barrier for heating systems, mid-price (€4-7/m). For most residential, multilayer offers best balance.

Per CTE DB-HS 4: Main supply from meter: 22-28mm minimum. Main distribution in house: 18-22mm. Branch to bathroom group: 16-18mm. Individual fixtures: 12-14mm (sinks), 14-16mm (showers, toilets). Hot water circulation return: same as supply diameter. Undersized pipes cause pressure drop; oversized waste money and slow hot water delivery.

Three methods: Crimp (anillas de crimpado) - slide copper ring over PEX, insert fitting, crimp with tool. Clamp (abrazaderas) - use stainless steel clamp with special tool. Push-fit (conexión rápida) - push pipe onto fitting, internal teeth grip pipe. Crimp is most common in Spain. Always use manufacturer-matched fittings and tools. Test at 6 bar before concealing.

Hot water pipes must be insulated per CTE DB-HS 4 (energy efficiency). Minimum 13mm foam insulation for indoor, 19mm for outdoor/unheated spaces. Cold water pipes should be insulated in unheated spaces to prevent freezing, and in humid areas to prevent condensation. Insulation costs €1-2/m, pays for itself in 2-3 years through energy savings.

Normal operating pressure is 3-4 bar (45-60 PSI). Minimum for proper fixture operation is 1.5 bar. Maximum per Spanish regulations is 6 bar. If street pressure exceeds 6 bar, install pressure reducing valve (válvula reductora de presión) at meter. Low pressure solutions: booster pump (grupo de presión) or accumulator tank (depósito acumulador).
