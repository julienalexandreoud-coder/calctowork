What is the Concrete Beams Calculator?

The Concrete Beams Calculator determines total reinforced concrete volume for horizontal structural beams spanning between columns in building frames. Construction teams throughout Spain use this tool when ordering concrete and planning formwork for vigas that support floor slabs, roof loads, and superimposed loads in residential, commercial, and industrial structures.

You may also find the Concrete Column Calculator, Joist and Slab Calculator, and Reinforced Concrete Calculator useful.

Beams serve as primary flexural members in reinforced concrete construction. A typical residential primary beam measures 250 mm wide by 400-500 mm deep (canto). Commercial buildings require larger beams: 300×600 mm, 400×700 mm, or transfer beams exceeding 500×1200 mm. Beam spans vary from 4-5 m in residential construction to 8-12 m in commercial applications.

This calculator processes four inputs: width, depth (canto), length, and quantity. The quantity parameter enables total volume calculation for multiple identical beams throughout a building. Accurate beam volume estimation ensures proper concrete delivery scheduling and coordinates with slab pouring sequences.

Formula Explained with Actual Calculations

Volume per Beam (m³) = Width (m) × Depth (m) × Length (m)

Total Volume (m³) = Volume per Beam × Quantity

The formula calculates each beam's volume as a rectangular prism. Depth (canto) represents total vertical dimension. When beams are monolithic with slabs (T-beam construction), use net rib depth (total depth minus slab thickness) to avoid double-counting slab concrete in separate calculations.

Input Variables:

  • Width (b): Horizontal beam width in meters. Example: 0.25 m for 250 mm beam.
  • Depth / Canto (h): Total vertical depth in meters. Example: 0.45 m for 450 mm beam.
  • Length (L): Clear span length between supports in meters. Example: 5.0 m span.
  • Quantity: Number of identical beams. Example: 6 primary beams per floor.

Typical Beam Sizes by Application:

  • Residential secondary beams: 200×300 mm to 250×400 mm
  • Residential primary beams: 250×400 mm to 300×500 mm
  • Commercial beams (5-7 m span): 300×500 mm to 400×700 mm
  • Transfer beams: 400×800 mm to 600×1500 mm
  • Grade beams: 300×400 mm to 400×600 mm
  • Lintel beams: 200×200 mm to 250×300 mm

Step-by-Step Calculation Process

Step 1: Extract beam dimensions from structural frame drawings. Drawing schedule might show: "V-1: 25×45 cm, L=5.2 m, V-2: 30×60 cm, L=6.5 m"

Step 2: Determine if depth includes slab thickness. For monolithic construction with 120 mm slab and 450 mm total depth, net rib depth = 450 - 120 = 330 mm.

Step 3: Convert dimensions to meters: 25 cm = 0.25 m, 45 cm = 0.45 m, 5.2 m stays as is.

Step 4: Calculate volume per beam: 0.25 m × 0.45 m × 5.2 m = 0.585 m³.

Step 5: Count beams from floor plan: 6 beams of type V-1 on this floor.

Step 6: Calculate total: 0.585 m³ × 6 = 3.51 m³. Add 5% waste: 3.51 × 1.05 = 3.69 m³.

Real Examples with Specific Numbers

Example 1: Primary Beam in Residential Floor

A residential building in Valencia has 3 primary beams spanning between columns. Each beam measures 250 mm wide, 450 mm total depth (canto), with 5.0 m span length. Beams are monolithic with 120 mm thick slab. HA-25 concrete specified with B500S reinforcement.

Calculation:

Full depth volume: 0.25 × 0.45 × 5.0 = 0.5625 m³ per beam

Total for 3 beams: 0.5625 × 3 = 1.6875 m³

Net rib depth (if slab calculated separately): 0.45 - 0.12 = 0.33 m

Net volume: 0.25 × 0.33 × 5.0 × 3 = 1.2375 m³

Material Order: Order 2.0 m³ of HA-25 at €92/m³ = €184 if pouring beams with slab (use full depth). If slab poured separately, order 1.5 m³ for beam ribs only. Beams require bottom reinforcement (3 Ø16 typical) for positive moment and top reinforcement over supports (2 Ø16) for negative moment. Stirrups: Ø8 at 150 mm spacing.

Example 2: Transfer Beam in Commercial Building

A commercial building in Madrid has a single transfer beam carrying loads from three floors above. The beam measures 500 mm wide, 1200 mm deep, spanning 8.0 m between two large columns. Design load: approximately 2,500 kN. HA-35 concrete specified with heavy reinforcement.

Calculation:

Volume: 0.50 × 1.20 × 8.0 = 4.80 m³

With 5% waste: 4.80 × 1.05 = 5.04 m³

Material Order: Order 5.0 m³ of HA-35 at €108/m³ = €540. This heavily reinforced beam requires multiple rebar layers: 5 Ø25 bottom bars, 4 Ø25 top bars, possibly 3-4 layers total. Stirrups: Ø12 at 100 mm spacing near supports, 150 mm at midspan. Formwork must withstand 28.8 kN/m² lateral pressure (24 kN/m³ × 1.2 m depth). Use tie rods (tirantes) Ø16 at 600 mm spacing to prevent formwork blowout.

Example 3: Secondary Beams (Joists) in Office Floor

An office floor has 12 secondary beams spanning between primary beams. Each secondary beam measures 200 mm wide, 350 mm deep (net rib, excluding 120 mm slab), with 6.0 m span. HA-25 concrete specified.

Calculation:

Volume per beam: 0.20 × 0.35 × 6.0 = 0.42 m³

Total for 12 beams: 0.42 × 12 = 5.04 m³

With 5% waste: 5.04 × 1.05 = 5.29 m³

Material Order: Order 5.5 m³ of HA-25 at €92/m³ = €506. Pour secondary beams monolithically with floor slab in single operation. Total slab + beam pour might reach 40-50 m³ requiring 5-6 truck deliveries. Secondary beam reinforcement: 3 Ø14 bottom bars, 2 Ø12 top bars, Ø6 stirrups at 200 mm spacing. Steel weight approximately 45 kg per beam.

Example 4: Grade Beams Connecting Isolated Footings

A residential building foundation has grade beams (vigas de atado) connecting isolated footings. Total grade beam length: 45 meters. Beam cross-section: 300 mm wide, 400 mm deep. Beams rest on compacted soil with 100 mm blinding layer. HA-25 concrete specified.

Calculation:

Volume: 0.30 × 0.40 × 45.0 = 5.40 m³

With 5% waste: 5.40 × 1.05 = 5.67 m³

Material Order: Order 6.0 m³ of HA-25 at €92/m³ = €552. Grade beams typically reinforce with 4 Ø14 longitudinal bars (2 top, 2 bottom) and Ø8 stirrups at 200 mm spacing. Steel weight: approximately 65 kg/m × 45 m = 2,925 kg at €1.35/kg = €3,949. Excavate beam trenches 500 mm wider than beam for formwork access. Pour blinding layer first, then install reinforcement and formwork.

Example 5: Mixed Beam Types in Single Floor

A commercial floor has multiple beam types: Primary beams V-1 (4 units, 30×60 cm, 7.0 m span), Secondary beams V-2 (8 units, 25×40 cm, 5.5 m span), Edge beams V-3 (perimeter 40 m, 25×50 cm). All beams monolithic with 150 mm slab. HA-30 concrete specified.

Calculation (using net rib depth):

V-1 net depth: 0.60 - 0.15 = 0.45 m. Volume: 0.30 × 0.45 × 7.0 × 4 = 3.78 m³

V-2 net depth: 0.40 - 0.15 = 0.25 m. Volume: 0.25 × 0.25 × 5.5 × 8 = 2.75 m³

V-3 net depth: 0.50 - 0.15 = 0.35 m. Volume: 0.25 × 0.35 × 40.0 = 3.50 m³

Grand total: 3.78 + 2.75 + 3.50 = 10.03 m³

Material Order: Order 10.5 m³ of HA-30 at €98/m³ = €1,029 for beam ribs. Slab calculated separately. Pour all beams and slab in single continuous operation to create monolithic floor diaphragm. Schedule 2 truck deliveries at 20-minute intervals. Total floor concrete (beams + slab) might reach 35-40 m³.

Common Mistakes

  • Double-counting slab concrete in T-beam construction: When beams are monolithic with slabs, the slab portion within beam depth gets counted in both beam and slab calculations. Use net rib depth (total depth minus slab thickness) for beam-only calculations. A 450 mm deep beam with 120 mm slab has 330 mm net rib depth, not 450 mm.
  • Using center-to-center span instead of clear span: Beam length should be clear span between support faces, not center-to-center column distance. For 400 mm columns at 5.0 m center-to-center, clear span = 5.0 - 0.40 = 4.60 m. Using 5.0 m overestimates volume by 9%.
  • Ignoring beam haunches at supports: Some beams feature haunches (deepened sections) near supports for increased negative moment capacity. A 200 mm deep haunch over 1.0 m length at each end adds 0.25 × 0.20 × 1.0 × 2 = 0.10 m³ per beam. For 10 beams: 1.0 m³ additional concrete often omitted.
  • Not accounting for beam drops in flat slab construction: Flat slab construction uses drop panels around columns that function as short, wide beams. A 2.0×2.0 m drop panel with 200 mm additional depth adds 0.80 m³ per column. Eight columns add 6.4 m³—significant volume requiring separate calculation.

Pro Tips from Beam Construction Specialists

Tip 1: Design beam formwork soffits (bottom forms) to carry full wet concrete weight with adequate propping. For beams deeper than 600 mm, install tie rods (tirantes) Ø12-16 mm through beam sides at 600 mm vertical spacing. Lateral pressure from wet concrete: 24 kN/m³ × depth. A 1.0 m deep beam exerts 24 kN/m² (2.4 tonnes/m²) on side formwork.

Tip 2: Strike (remove) beam formwork only after concrete reaches specified strength. Side forms: 24-48 hours for HA-25/30. Bottom props under beams: minimum 14 days for spans up to 6 m, 21 days for spans over 6 m. Early prop removal causes excessive deflection and potential structural damage. Test field-cured cylinders before stripping.

Tip 3: Pour beams before slabs when using different concrete strengths. If columns require HA-35 but beams need only HA-25, pour columns first, then beams and slabs together at HA-25. Use mesh separator at beam-column joint or pour joint zone at higher strength. Coordinate deliveries to maintain continuous placement without cold joints.

Tip 4: Install beam reinforcement with proper bar spacing. Minimum clear spacing between bars: 25 mm or bar diameter, whichever is greater. For congested beams, use bundled bars (maximum 4 bars per bundle). Ensure concrete cover: 30-35 mm for interior beams, 40-45 mm for exterior exposure. Use plastic spacer chairs at 600 mm intervals.

Tip 5: Provide camber (pre-deflection) in formwork for long-span beams (>8 m). Wet concrete weight causes formwork deflection. Install formwork with 10-15 mm upward camber at midspan for 8-10 m spans. After concrete hardens and props are removed, beam settles to level position. Without camber, finished beam appears sagged.

Frequently Asked Questions

Rule of thumb for preliminary sizing: Simply supported beams: depth = span/10 to span/12. Continuous beams: depth = span/12 to span/15. For 5 m span: simply supported = 400-500 mm depth, continuous = 330-420 mm depth. These proportions control deflection; structural engineer must verify for bending moment and shear capacity. Final depth depends on reinforcement ratio, concrete strength, and applied loads.
