What is the Joist Slab (Forjado de Vigueta) Calculator?

The Joist Slab Calculator determines concrete volume and joist quantities for traditional Spanish one-way ribbed slab systems (forjado de vigueta). Construction teams throughout Spain use this tool when estimating materials for viguetas (precast joists), bovedillas (hollow blocks), and compression slab concrete in residential and commercial floor systems.

You may also find the Concrete Beam Calculator, Concrete Slab Calculator, and Concrete Column Calculator useful.

Forjado de vigueta represents the most common floor system in Spanish residential construction. The system combines precast concrete joists (viguetas) spaced at 600-700 mm centers, hollow filler blocks (bovedillas) typically made of clay or lightweight concrete, and an in-situ compression layer (capa de compresión) 40-50 mm thick with welded wire mesh.

This calculator processes four inputs: slab length, slab width, joist spacing (intereje), and slab depth (canto del forjado). Results include total concrete volume for both joists and compression layer, plus the number of viguetas required. Accurate estimation prevents material shortages during critical floor construction phases.

Formula Explained with Actual Calculations

Number of Joists = (Width / Joist Spacing) + 1

Joist Concrete Volume = Number of Joists × Joist Width × Slab Depth × Length

Compression Slab Volume = Length × Width × Compression Layer Thickness

Total Volume = Joist Volume + Compression Slab Volume

The calculator determines joist count based on slab width and center-to-center spacing (intereje). Joist concrete volume accounts for precast vigueta concrete. Compression slab volume calculates in-situ concrete poured over joists and bovedillas. Hollow blocks (bovedillas) contain no concrete—they are separate purchased items.

Input Variables:

  • Length (L): Span direction (joist direction) in meters. Example: 5.0 m.
  • Width (W): Perpendicular dimension in meters. Example: 4.0 m.
  • Joist Spacing / Intereje: Center-to-center distance between joists. Standard: 0.60 m or 0.70 m.
  • Slab Depth / Canto: Total floor system depth including joists and compression layer. Typical: 0.20 m, 0.25 m, or 0.30 m.

Standard Forjado Specifications:

  • Intereje 600 mm: Joist width 120 mm, block width 480 mm
  • Intereje 700 mm: Joist width 140 mm, block width 560 mm
  • Compression layer: 40 mm minimum, 50 mm recommended
  • Canto 200 mm: Spans up to 5.5 m
  • Canto 250 mm: Spans 5.5-7.0 m
  • Canto 300 mm: Spans 7.0-8.5 m

Step-by-Step Calculation Process

Step 1: Measure floor dimensions from architectural plans. Bedroom might measure 5.0 m (span direction) by 4.0 m (perpendicular).

Step 2: Identify intereje from structural drawings. Standard residential: 600 mm (0.60 m).

Step 3: Determine total canto from span requirements. For 5.0 m span: 200 mm (0.20 m) typical.

Step 4: Calculate joist count: (4.0 / 0.60) + 1 = 6.67 + 1 = 7.67, round up to 8 joists.

Step 5: Calculate joist volume: 8 joists × 0.12 m × 0.20 m × 5.0 m = 0.96 m³.

Step 6: Calculate compression slab: 5.0 × 4.0 × 0.04 = 0.80 m³. Total: 0.96 + 0.80 = 1.76 m³.

Real Examples with Specific Numbers

Example 1: Residential Bedroom Floor

A bedroom in Valencia measures 5.0 m by 4.0 m. The forjado system uses viguetas with 600 mm intereje and 200 mm total canto. Compression layer is 40 mm thick. Joist width is 120 mm. Bovedillas measure 440×250×200 mm (clay blocks).

Calculation:

Number of joists: (4.0 / 0.60) + 1 = 7.67, round up to 8 joists

Joist volume: 8 × 0.12 × 0.20 × 5.0 = 0.96 m³

Compression slab: 5.0 × 4.0 × 0.04 = 0.80 m³

Total concrete: 0.96 + 0.80 = 1.76 m³

Bovedillas count: 8 joist spaces × (5.0 / 0.44) = 8 × 11.36 = 91 blocks per layer, 2 layers = 182 bovedillas

Material Order: Order 2.0 m³ of HA-25 concrete for compression layer at €92/m³ = €184. Order 8 viguetas at €12-15/m = 8 × 5.0 m × €13.50/m = €540. Order 182 bovedillas at €1.80 each = €327.60. Add A-200 welded mesh (mallazo) for compression layer: 20 m² × €3.50/m² = €70. Total: €1,121.60.

Example 2: Large Living Room Floor

Calculation:

Number of joists: (6.0 / 0.70) + 1 = 9.57, round up to 10 joists

Joist volume: 10 × 0.14 × 0.25 × 8.0 = 2.80 m³

Compression slab: 8.0 × 6.0 × 0.05 = 2.40 m³

Total concrete: 2.80 + 2.40 = 5.20 m³

Bovedillas count: 10 spaces × (8.0 / 0.52) = 10 × 15.38 = 154 blocks per layer, 2 layers = 308 bovedillas

Material Order: Order 5.5 m³ of HA-25 concrete at €92/m³ = €506. Order 10 viguetas (8.0 m each) at €14/m = 10 × 8.0 × €14 = €1,120. Order 308 bovedillas (520×250×250 mm) at €2.20 each = €677.60. Mallazo A-200: 48 m² × €3.50/m² = €168. Install temporary propping (apeo) at 1.5 m spacing along joist span, leave 14 days after pouring.

Example 3: Complete House Floor (Multiple Rooms)

A house floor plan includes: Bedroom 1 (4.0×3.5 m), Bedroom 2 (4.0×3.5 m), Living room (6.0×5.0 m), Kitchen (4.0×3.0 m), Bathroom (2.5×2.0 m). All rooms use 600 mm intereje, 200 mm canto, 40 mm compression layer. Total floor area: 78 m².

Pour compression layer in single operation for monolithic diaphragm action. Use concrete pump for efficient placement.

Common Mistakes

  • Forgetting compression layer volume: The capa de compresión represents 30-50% of total concrete volume. A 200 mm forjado with 40 mm compression layer has 20% of concrete in compression layer. Omitting this results in significant material shortage mid-pour.
  • Not rounding up joist count: Joist count must round up to whole number. You cannot install 7.3 joists. For 4.0 m width at 600 mm intereje: (4.0/0.60)+1 = 7.67, round up to 8 joists. Using 7 joists increases spacing to 667 mm, compromising structural capacity and violating design specifications.
  • Confusing intereje with clear spacing: Intereje is center-to-center distance, not clear gap between joists. With 600 mm intereje and 120 mm joist width, clear gap = 600 - 120 = 480 mm (bovedilla width). Ordering 600 mm wide bovedillas creates installation problems.
  • Not accounting for joist bearing length: Precast viguetas require 100-150 mm minimum bearing on supports. Ordering 5.0 m viguetas for 5.0 m span leaves zero bearing. Order 5.2-5.3 m viguetas for 5.0 m clear span. Bearing length affects ordering length but not concrete volume calculation.

Pro Tips from Forjado Specialists

Tip 1: Verify fire resistance rating (CTE requirements) before specifying forjado. Standard 200 mm forjado with clay bovedillas and 40 mm compression provides REI 60 (60 minutes). REI 90 requires 250 mm canto or additional fire protection. REI 120 requires 300 mm canto or applied fireproofing. Document fire rating for building permit approval.

Tip 2: Install temporary propping (apeo) immediately after placing viguetas and bovedillas. Props should be Ø48 mm steel tubes spaced at maximum 1.50 m along joist span. Never walk on unpropped forjado—viguetas can crack under construction loads before compression layer cures. Leave props in place minimum 14 days after pouring compression layer.

Tip 3: Place mallazo (welded mesh) in upper third of compression layer, approximately 15-20 mm below finished surface. This position controls shrinkage cracking effectively. Use plastic spacer chairs (sillas) at 1 m intervals to maintain mesh position during concrete placement. Mallazo should overlap 200 mm at sheet edges with wire ties.

Tip 4: Pour compression layer in single continuous operation for each floor. Cold joints within compression layer compromise diaphragm action and create crack paths. For large floors (over 100 m³), divide into bays using temporary formwork, but complete each bay in single pour. Schedule concrete deliveries at 15-20 minute intervals.

Tip 5: Cure compression layer minimum 7 days before removing props. Apply curing compound or cover with wet burlap and plastic sheeting within 30 minutes of finishing. Uncured compression layers develop surface crazing and reduced strength. In summer, pour early morning (6-9 AM) to extend working time before initial set.

Frequently Asked Questions

Viguetas are precast reinforced concrete joists providing structural strength. They span between supports and carry floor loads. Viguetas contain steel reinforcement (prestressed or conventional) and cost €12-18/m depending on span and load capacity. Bovedillas are hollow filler blocks (clay, expanded clay, or lightweight concrete) placed between viguetas. They are non-structural, reducing floor weight while providing thermal/acoustic insulation. Bovedillas cost €1.50-2.50 each depending on size and material.

600 mm intereje is standard for residential construction, accommodating 440 mm wide bovedillas with 120 mm viguetas. 700 mm intereje reduces joist count (lower cost) but requires wider 520 mm bovedillas and thicker compression layer. Structural engineer specifies intereje based on span, loads, and forjado manufacturer's system. Do not change intereje without engineer approval—it affects structural capacity.

Minimum 40 mm for residential floors with normal loads. 50 mm recommended for areas with heavier loads, spans over 6 m, or where underfloor heating pipes embed in slab. Commercial floors may require 60-70 mm compression layer. Compression layer must include welded mesh (mallazo electrosoldado) A-200 for residential, A-300 or A-400 for commercial. Mesh provides crack control and composite action with viguetas.

Standard vigueta systems limit spans to 6-7 m maximum. For longer spans, use prestressed viguetas (viguetas pretensadas) spanning up to 10-12 m, or alternative systems: solid slab (losa maciza), waffle slab (forjado reticular), or post-tensioned slab. Prestressed viguetas cost 30-40% more than conventional but enable column-free spaces. Structural engineer determines appropriate system based on architectural requirements and budget.
