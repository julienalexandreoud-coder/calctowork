4 Enthalpy FAQs

Enthalpy (H) measures heat content ‚ÄĒ the total energy in a system available for heat transfer. Entropy (S) measures disorder or randomness. Standard enthalpy of formation (őĒH¬įf) is defined relative to elements in their most stable form at 25¬įC and 1 bar pressure. By convention, these reference states have őĒH¬įf = 0. Oxygen gas (O‚āā), nitrogen gas (N‚āā), carbon as graphite (C), and iron metal (Fe) all have zero formation enthalpy.
