What is the Tile Cladding Calculator?

The Tile Cladding Calculator determines the number of tiles, grout, and adhesive required for tiling wall surfaces (alicatado). Construction teams throughout Spain use this tool when estimating materials for wall tiling in bathrooms, kitchens, swimming pools, and commercial spaces where waterproof, durable, and easy-to-clean surfaces are essential.

Wall tiling applications vary by room and tile type. Bathroom walls typically use 200×300 mm ceramic tiles with 3 mm grout joints. Kitchen backsplashes often feature smaller 100×100 mm tiles with 2 mm joints. Commercial kitchens and swimming pools require porcelain or mosaic tiles with epoxy grout for chemical resistance and waterproofing.

This calculator processes four inputs: wall length, wall height, tile size, and grout joint width. Results include total tile count with waste allowance, grout volume, and adhesive quantity. Accurate estimation prevents material shortages during tiling and avoids color variation from different production batches.

Formula Explained with Actual Calculations

Wall Area (m²) = Length (m) × Height (m)

Tiles per m² = 1 / ((Tile Length + Joint) × (Tile Width + Joint))

Total Tiles = Wall Area × Tiles per m² × 1.10 (10% waste)

Tile count based on effective tile size including grout joint. 10% waste allowance standard for straight-lay patterns. Diagonal or herringbone patterns require 15-20% waste allowance due to increased cutting. Grout volume calculated from joint width, joint depth (tile thickness), and total joint length.

Input Variables:

  • Length (L): Horizontal wall length to be tiled in meters. Example: 2.50 m for bathroom wall.
  • Height (H): Vertical wall height to be tiled in meters. Example: 2.40 m full height.
  • Tile Size: Individual tile dimensions in millimeters. Example: 200×300 mm ceramic.
  • Grout Joint (junta): Grout joint width in millimeters. Typical: 2-5 mm for wall tiles.

Standard Wall Tile Specifications:

  • Bathroom walls: 200×300 mm, 3 mm joints, ceramic
  • Kitchen backsplash: 100×100 mm, 2 mm joints, ceramic
  • Large format walls: 300×600 mm, 3 mm joints, porcelain
  • Mosaic sheets: 300×300 mm sheets, 2 mm joints
  • Commercial kitchens: 200×200 mm, 5 mm joints, porcelain
  • Swimming pools: 50×50 mm mosaic, 2 mm joints, glass

Step-by-Step Calculation Process

Step 1: Measure wall dimensions from architectural plans or on-site. Drawing might specify: "Alicatado A-1: L=2.50 m, H=2.40 m"

Step 2: Calculate gross area: 2.50 m × 2.40 m = 6.00 m².

Step 3: Deduct openings (windows, doors). For 1 window (0.80×1.00 m): 0.80 × 1.00 = 0.80 m².

Step 4: Net area: 6.00 − 0.80 = 5.20 m².

Step 5: Calculate tiles per m²: (0.200 + 0.003) × (0.300 + 0.003) = 0.0615 m². 1 / 0.0615 = 16.26 tiles/m².

Step 6: Calculate total: 5.20 × 16.26 = 84.6 tiles + 10% waste = 94 tiles.

Common Mistakes

  • Not accounting for pattern waste: Straight-lay patterns require 10% waste allowance. Diagonal, herringbone, or patterned layouts require 15-20% waste due to increased cutting at edges. For diagonal layout on 20 m²: 20% waste = 4 m² additional tiles. Using 10% for diagonal pattern creates mid-job shortage. Always match waste allowance to pattern complexity.
  • Forgetting to deduct openings: Windows, doors, and niches reduce tiled area. Always deduct these from gross wall area before calculating tile quantities. For 20 m² wall with 3 m² openings: deduct 15% from tile order. Failing to deduct results in over-ordering and wasted money on unused tiles.
  • Using wrong adhesive trowel size: Notched trowel size determines adhesive consumption and bond strength. Small tiles (up to 200 mm): 4-6 mm trowel, 3-4 kg/m². Medium tiles (200-400 mm): 6-8 mm trowel, 4-6 kg/m². Large tiles (over 400 mm): 8-12 mm trowel, 6-10 kg/m². Using wrong trowel causes bond failure or excessive adhesive costs.
  • Not checking tile batch consistency: Tiles from different production batches may have slight color variations. Order all tiles from same batch number. Keep spare tiles from same batch for future repairs. For large orders, verify batch numbers on all boxes before installation. Color variation between batches becomes obvious after installation and cannot be corrected.

Pro Tips from Tiling Specialists

Tip 1: Always dry-lay tiles before applying adhesive. Place tiles without adhesive to check layout and identify where cuts will fall. Avoid narrow slivers of tile at edges—adjust starting point so cut tiles at both ends are at least half tile wide. For 2.50 m wall with 300 mm tiles: 8 full tiles = 2.40 m, leaving 100 mm at each end (better than 25 mm at one end and 375 mm at other).

Tip 2: Use waterproof membrane behind tiles in wet areas (showers, bath surrounds). Apply liquid-applied membrane or sheet membrane to substrate before tiling. Membrane cost: €20-30/m² installed. Skipping membrane in shower areas causes water damage to wall structure within 2-5 years. Spanish building codes (CTE) require waterproofing in all wet areas.

Tip 3: Use flexible, waterproof grout in wet areas. Epoxy grout completely waterproof, stain-resistant, doesn't require sealing—ideal for showers, pools, commercial kitchens. Polymer-modified cementitious grout sufficient for residential bathroom walls. Standard cementitious grout acceptable for dry areas only. Grout cost difference: cementitious €8/5kg, epoxy €35/5kg—worth the investment for wet areas.

Tip 4: Seal natural stone tiles before and after grouting to prevent staining. Natural stone (marble, travertine, slate) is porous and absorbs grout, causing permanent staining. Apply penetrating sealer before grouting, clean excess grout immediately, apply second sealer coat after grout cures. Sealer cost: €25-40/liter, coverage 10-15 m²/liter. Skipping sealer ruins natural stone appearance.

Tip 5: Allow proper curing time before grouting. Adhesive must cure minimum 24 hours before grouting (48 hours for large tiles or cool conditions). Grouting too soon disturbs tile bond and causes lippage. Test adhesive by pressing grout line—should be firm with no indentation. In humid conditions, extend curing to 48 hours. Full adhesive cure takes 14-28 days—avoid heavy loads during this period.

Frequently Asked Questions

Adhesive consumption depends on trowel size and tile type. Guide: 4 mm trowel = 3-4 kg/m² (small tiles), 6 mm trowel = 4-5 kg/m² (medium tiles), 8 mm trowel = 6-8 kg/m² (large tiles), 10 mm trowel = 8-10 kg/m² (extra-large tiles). For large-format tiles (over 600 mm), use back-buttering technique (apply adhesive to both wall and tile back), increasing consumption 20-30%. Always use flexible adhesive (C2 class) for large tiles and wet areas.

Grout consumption depends on tile size and joint width. Guide: 200×200 mm tiles with 3 mm joints = 0.4-0.5 kg/m², 300×600 mm tiles with 3 mm joints = 0.2-0.3 kg/m², 100×100 mm tiles with 2 mm joints = 0.5-0.6 kg/m². Smaller tiles and wider joints use more grout. Formula: Grout (kg/m²) = (tile length + tile width) × joint width × joint depth × 1.6 / (tile length × tile width), where 1.6 is grout density in kg/dm³.

Cementitious grout: standard choice for most wall tiling. Easy to apply, many colors, cost-effective (€8-12/5kg). Epoxy grout: more expensive (€30-45/5kg), harder to work with, but completely waterproof, stain-resistant, no sealing required. Use epoxy grout in wet areas (showers, pools), commercial kitchens, and chemical exposure areas. For residential bathroom walls, polymer-modified cementitious grout usually sufficient.
