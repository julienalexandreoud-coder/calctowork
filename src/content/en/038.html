What is the Underfloor Heating Calculator?

The underfloor heating calculator determines the pipe length, number of circuits, and manifold ports required for wet underfloor heating (suelo radiante por agua) installations. It calculates pipe spacing, circuit lengths, and manifold sizing based on heated area, helping contractors design efficient low-temperature heating systems compatible with condensing boilers and heat pumps.

You may also find the Aluminium Radiator Calculator, Copper and PEX Pipe Calculator, and Gas Boiler Calculator useful.

In Spanish construction, underfloor heating follows RITE (Reglamento de Instalaciones Térmicas en los Edificios) and UNE-EN 1264 standards. The calculator helps contractors working on new construction (obra nueva), renovations (reformas), and luxury homes determine PEX-AL-PEX or PEX pipe quantities, insulation boards (paneles aislantes), and manifold (colector) configurations for comfortable, efficient heating.

Formula Explained

L_pipe = Área / Espaciado   |   N° circuitos = L_pipe / Longitud_max_circuito   |   Puertos_colector = N° circuitos

Where Área is the heated floor area in m², Espaciado is pipe spacing (typically 15-25 cm), Longitud_max_circuito is maximum circuit length (80-120 m depending on pipe diameter), and Puertos_colector is the number of manifold ports needed.

Standard spacing: 15 cm for bathrooms and high-heat-loss areas, 20 cm for standard rooms, 25 cm for well-insulated spaces. Pipe length per m²: 15 cm spacing = 6.7 m/m², 20 cm = 5 m/m², 25 cm = 4 m/m².

Step-by-Step Calculation Process

  1. Measure heated area: Calculate net floor area in m². Subtract fixed furniture (kitchen units, built-in wardrobes) where pipes cannot be installed. Typically 85-90% of room area is usable for pipes.
  2. Determine pipe spacing: Bathrooms and perimeter zones: 15 cm spacing. Standard rooms: 20 cm spacing. Well-insulated rooms: 25 cm spacing. Closer spacing = more even heat but more pipe and higher cost.
  3. Calculate total pipe length: Multiply area by pipe length per m² based on spacing. For 100 m² at 20 cm: 100 × 5 m/m² = 500 m. Add 10-15% for manifold connections and waste.
  4. Determine circuit count: Divide total pipe length by maximum circuit length. For 550 m total with 100 m max circuits: 550 / 100 = 5.5 → 6 circuits. Each circuit needs 2 manifold ports (flow and return).
  5. Size the manifold: Manifold ports = number of circuits. Standard manifolds: 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 8, 10, 12 ports. For 6 circuits, use 6-port or 8-port manifold (spare ports for future expansion).
  6. Calculate insulation boards: Insulation thickness: 30 mm for ground floors, 20 mm for upper floors. Board size typically 1 m² or 1.2 m². Order 5-10% extra for cutting waste.

Common Mistakes

  • Exceeding maximum circuit length: Circuits over 120 m (16mm pipe) create excessive pressure drop, causing flow imbalance and cold spots. Pump cannot overcome friction. Always divide long runs into multiple circuits. For 200 m² area, use 8-10 circuits of 80-100 m each, not 4 circuits of 200 m.
  • Wrong pipe spacing for floor covering: Tile floors conduct heat well - 20-25 cm spacing works. Wood/laminate floors insulate - need 15-20 cm spacing. Thick carpet (not recommended) may need 10-15 cm spacing. Wrong spacing causes uneven heating or floor damage.
  • Insufficient insulation below pipes: Ground floors without adequate insulation (30-50 mm) lose 20-30% of heat downward. Upper floors need 20-30 mm to prevent heating ceiling below. Cheap insulation saves €500-1,000 initially but costs €200-400/year in wasted energy.
  • Not accounting for furniture: Pipes under fixed furniture (kitchen cabinets, built-in wardrobes, bathtubs) waste heat and can damage furniture. Plan pipe layout to avoid fixed installations. Reduce heated area by 10-15% for typical furniture coverage.

Pro Tips from Specialists

  1. Install pipe layout software before construction: Use CAD software or manufacturer layout tools to plan pipe runs. Optimise circuit lengths (all circuits within 10% of each other for balanced flow). Mark manifold location, circuit boundaries, and pipe paths on floor before installation. Prevents costly mistakes and material waste.
  2. Use oxygen-barrier pipe: PEX-AL-PEX or PEX-EVOH pipe has oxygen barrier preventing corrosion in boiler and radiators. Standard PEX without barrier allows oxygen diffusion, corroding ferrous components. Oxygen-barrier pipe costs €0.10-0.20/m more but protects entire heating system. Essential for mixed radiator/underfloor systems.
  3. Install flowmeters on manifold: Manifold with flowmeters (caudalímetros, €150-250 premium) allows precise circuit balancing during commissioning. Without flowmeters, balancing is trial-and-error. Flowmeters pay for themselves in reduced commissioning time and optimal system performance.
  4. Pressure test before screed: Pressurise system to 6 bar before pouring screed (solera). Maintain pressure during screed pour and curing. Monitor for 48 hours - any pressure drop indicates leak. Fixing leaks after screed is catastrophic (€5,000-15,000 to locate and repair). Test protocol: 6 bar for 24 hours, max 0.2 bar drop acceptable.
  5. Allow proper curing before heating: Wait 21-28 days after screed pour before first heating. First heat-up must be gradual: start at 20°C water temperature, increase 5°C per day to operating temperature. Rapid heating causes screed cracking. Document heating protocol for warranty compliance.
