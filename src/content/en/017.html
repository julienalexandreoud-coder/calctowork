What is the Brick Partition Wall Calculator?

The Brick Partition Wall Calculator determines the number of bricks and mortar volume required for constructing internal partition walls (tabiques de ladrillo). Construction teams throughout Spain use this tool when estimating materials for non-load-bearing interior walls that divide spaces into rooms, corridors, and functional areas in residential and commercial buildings.

Partition walls (tabiques) in Spanish construction typically use single-layer brickwork (half-brick thick, 115 mm or 120 mm). Standard clay bricks measure 240×115×70 mm, laid with 10 mm mortar joints. Unlike exterior walls, partition walls don't require damp proof courses but must tie into structural elements for lateral stability.

This calculator processes three inputs: length, height, and thickness. Results include total wall volume, brick count based on standard dimensions, and mortar volume required for laying. Accurate estimation prevents material shortages during interior construction phases and ensures proper cement and sand ordering.

Formula Explained with Actual Calculations

Wall Volume (m³) = Length (m) × Height (m) × Thickness (m)

Number of Bricks = Wall Volume / (Brick Volume with Mortar Joints)

Mortar Volume (m³) ≈ Wall Volume × 0.20 to 0.25

Partition walls require higher mortar percentage (20-25%) than thicker walls because joints represent larger proportion of total volume in thin walls. Standard Spanish brick dimensions 240×115×70 mm with 10 mm joints produce effective unit size 250×125×80 mm.

Input Variables:

  • Length (L): Total horizontal partition wall length in meters. Example: 3.5 m for bathroom wall.
  • Height (H): Vertical wall height in meters. Example: 2.70 m (floor-to-ceiling).
  • Thickness (T): Wall thickness in meters. Example: 0.115 m (half-brick tabique).

Standard Partition Wall Specifications:

  • Half-brick tabique: 115 mm thick, 60 bricks/m²
  • Perforated brick tabique: 110 mm thick, 55 bricks/m²
  • Lightweight block partition: 75-100 mm thick, 6-8 blocks/m²
  • Standard height: 2.50-3.00 m (floor-to-ceiling)
  • Mortar ratio: 1:4 cement:sand for tabiques

Step-by-Step Calculation Process

Step 1: Measure partition wall dimensions from architectural plans. Drawing might specify: "Tabique T-1: L=3.5 m, H=2.70 m, espesor=115 mm"

Step 2: Verify dimensions are in meters. Convert: 115 mm = 0.115 m, 270 cm = 2.70 m.

Step 3: Calculate wall volume: 3.5 m × 2.70 m × 0.115 m = 1.087 m³.

Step 4: Calculate brick count: brick with joints = 0.25 × 0.125 × 0.08 = 0.0025 m³. Bricks = 1.087 / 0.0025 = 435 bricks.

Step 5: Add 5% waste: 435 × 1.05 = 457 bricks.

Step 6: Calculate mortar (25% for thin wall): 1.087 × 0.25 = 0.272 m³.

Common Mistakes

  • Not deducting door and window openings: Partition walls typically contain multiple door openings. Failing to deduct these results in over-ordering bricks by 10-20%. A standard door (0.90×2.10×0.115 m) removes 0.217 m³ from wall volume—approximately 87 bricks. Always measure and deduct all openings before calculating material quantities.
  • Using wrong brick count per square meter: Number of bricks per m² depends on brick size and joint thickness. For standard 240×115×70 mm bricks with 10 mm joints laid as half-brick wall: approximately 60 bricks/m². Using incorrect count (e.g., 50 or 70 bricks/m²) creates significant material estimation errors. Always calculate based on actual brick dimensions with joints.
  • Forgetting wall ties to structural elements: Partition walls must tie to structural columns, beams, and adjacent walls using galvanized steel ties or toothing. This requirement not included in volume calculation but essential for lateral stability. Standard spacing: 2.5 ties per m² of wall area. For 20 m² partition: 50 ties at €0.35 = €17.50—small cost but critical for seismic resistance.
  • Not accounting for plaster finish: Brick wall volume doesn't include plaster finish. If wall will be plastered both sides (typical), calculate plaster volume separately: wall area × plaster thickness × 2 sides. For 10 m² wall with 15 mm plaster both sides: 10 × 0.015 × 2 = 0.30 m³ plaster mortar. Plaster materials often forgotten in initial estimates.

Pro Tips from Partition Wall Specialists

Tip 1: Construct brick solera (base course) at least 3 courses high using solid bricks or concrete before starting hollow brick work. Base course provides level, solid foundation and improves damp resistance. Height: approximately 200-250 mm above finished floor. Especially important in bathrooms and kitchens where water exposure possible. Use 1:4 mortar for base course, ensure fully bedded joints.

Tip 2: Include reinforced concrete tie beam (vinculo) at mid-height for walls taller than 3.0 m. Tie beam typically 150×150 mm with 2 Ø10 longitudinal bars and Ø6 stirrups at 200 mm spacing. Tie beam provides lateral stability and prevents buckling in tall partitions. Cast tie beam after wall reaches mid-height, continue brickwork after 7-day cure. Required by CTE for walls exceeding 3.0 m unsupported height.

Tip 3: Use 1:4 cement:sand mortar for brickwork with plasticizer or lime additive for workability. Mortar consistency critical—too wet causes bricks to slide, too dry prevents proper bonding. Test mortar by making thumbprint—should hold impression without sagging. Mix mortar in small batches (30-45 minutes usable life). Discard mortar that begins to set—never retemper with water.

Tip 4: Check wall for plumb and alignment every 3-4 courses using spirit level and string line. Maximum permissible deviation: 5 mm per 3 m height. Out-of-plumb walls create problems for door installation and finishing. Use wooden battens as guides for straight walls. For long walls (over 4 m), set up intermediate guide points. Install wall ties as you build—don't retrofit after wall completion.

Tip 5: Allow partition walls to settle and dry minimum 7 days before plastering. Fresh masonry contains significant moisture from mortar. Plastering too soon traps moisture, causing plaster failure and potential mold growth. In humid conditions, extend drying period to 14 days. Use moisture meter to verify wall is ready for plastering (target: below 5% moisture content). Apply primer/sealer before plastering to control suction.
